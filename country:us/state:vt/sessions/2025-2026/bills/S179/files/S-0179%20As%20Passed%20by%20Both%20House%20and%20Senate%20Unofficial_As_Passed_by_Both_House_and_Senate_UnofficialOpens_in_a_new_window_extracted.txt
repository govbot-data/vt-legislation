7 § 4101. SHORT TITLE
8 This chapter may be cited as the “Vermont Uniform Disclaimer of Property
9 Interests Act.”
10 § 4102. DEFINITIONS
11 As used in this chapter:
12 (1) “Disclaimant” means the person to whom a disclaimed interest or
13 power would have passed had the disclaimer not been made.
14 (2) “Disclaimed interest” means the interest that would have passed to
15 the disclaimant had the disclaimer not been made.
16 (3) “Disclaimer” means the refusal to accept an interest in or power over
17 property.
18 (4) “Fiduciary” means a personal representative, trustee, agent acting
19 under a power of attorney, or other person authorized to act as a fiduciary with
20 respect to the property of another person.
21 (5) “Jointly held property” means property held in the name of two or
22 more persons under an arrangement in which all holders have concurrent

6 § 4113. WHEN DISCLAIMER BARRED OR LIMITED
7 (a) A disclaimer is barred by a written waiver of the right to disclaim.
8 (b) A disclaimer of an interest in property is barred if any of the following
9 events occur before the disclaimer becomes effective:
10 (1) the disclaimant accepts the interest sought to be disclaimed;
11 (2) the disclaimant voluntarily assigns, conveys, encumbers, pledges, or
12 transfers the interest sought to be disclaimed or contracts to do so; or
13 (3) a judicial sale of the interest sought to be disclaimed occurs.
14 (c) A disclaimer, in whole or part, of the future exercise of a power held in
15 a fiduciary capacity is not barred by its previous exercise.
16 (d) A disclaimer, in whole or part, of the future exercise of a power not
17 held in a fiduciary capacity is not barred by its previous exercise unless the
18 power is exercisable in favor of the disclaimant.
19 (e) A disclaimer is barred or limited if so provided by law other than this
20 chapter.

5 § 4114. TAX QUALIFIED DISCLAIMER
6 Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, if as a result of a
7 disclaimer or transfer the disclaimed or transferred interest is treated pursuant
8 to the provisions of the Internal Revenue Code, as may be amended, or any
9 regulations promulgated under it, as never having been transferred to the
10 disclaimant, then the disclaimer or transfer is effective as a disclaimer under
11 this chapter.
12 § 4115. RECORDING OF DISCLAIMER
13 If an instrument transferring an interest in or power over property subject to
14 a disclaimer is required or permitted by law to be filed, recorded, or registered,
15 then the disclaimer may be so filed, recorded, or registered. Failure to file,
16 record, or register the disclaimer does not affect its validity as between the
17 disclaimant and persons to whom the property interest or power passes by
18 reason of the disclaimer.
