State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 306
Offered by House Committee on Environment
House concurrent resolution honoring George Springston for his exemplary roles in environmental and
natural resources management
Whereas, for over three decades, George Springston has drawn upon his environmental, geological,
mapping, and modeling expertise, which he has continuously updated, as a basis for his diverse and interesting
career in the field and in the digital sphere, and
Whereas, George Springston earned a bachelor’s and a master’s degree from Clemson University and the
University of Massachusetts, respectively, and
Whereas, in 1991, he became an assistant wetlands coordinator at the Agency of Natural Resources, working
in this key area of environmental policy for the next five years, and
Whereas, in 1996, shortly after the initiation of the U.S. Geological Survey’s STATEMAP program, which
provides funding to the states for surficial and bedrock geologic mapping, George Springston changed his
professional focus to geologic mapping, initially as an independent contractor, and, since 2002, as a research
associate on the staff of Norwich University, and
Whereas, George Springston has coauthored over 40 geologic maps, and he was integral to the creation of
Vermont’s surficial geologic map unit descriptions and surficial mapping standards, and
Whereas, around the year 2000, he began monitoring and advising on landslide threats, developing an
expertise that the Vermont Geological Survey has found invaluable, and he compiled landslide inventories for
four counties, performed dozens of individual landslide assessments, and is currently creating the State’s first
statistically based landslide-susceptibility model, and
Whereas, the breadth of George Springston’s environmental expertise was evidenced when he advised the
Town of Plainfield on its flood recovery plans and when he testified before the House Committee on
Environment regarding proposed wetlands rules, and
Whereas, after 35 years, George Springston is concluding his innovative service on behalf of Vermont’s
environmental and natural resources, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly honors George Springston for his exemplary roles in environmental and natural
resources management, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to George Springston.
