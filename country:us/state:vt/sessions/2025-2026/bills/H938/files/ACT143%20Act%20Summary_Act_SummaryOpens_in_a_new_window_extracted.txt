Act No. 143 (H.938). An act relating to establishing the Vermont Homelessness Response
Continuum
Subjects: Human services; emergency housing
This act establishes the Vermont Homelessness Response Continuum (Program),
which shall be operated by the Office of Economic Opportunity. The purpose of the
Program is to establish a continuum of supports and services for households who are
experiencing homelessness or who are at risk of experiencing homelessness. The
Program consists of varying levels of services, including prevention and diversion
services, shelter services, specialized shelter services, and emergency housing services in
a hotel or motel setting. The Program also provides permanent supportive housing and
case management services to households across the Program, as appropriate. This act
describes Program eligibility, time limits, household responsibilities, and reporting
requirements. It provides an appeals process for Program applicants and participants
through the Human Services Board. It requires the Department for Children and Families
to adopt emergency rules for the Program’s operation by September 1, 2026, and
permanent rules by December 1, 2027. While the Program takes effect on July 1, 2026,
this act recognizes that the Office of Economic Opportunity and community partners will
not have capacity to fully implement the Program by that date and requires the Office to
implement the Program to the fullest extent of its ability in fiscal year 2027, while
developing capacity to fully implement the Program in fiscal year 2028. This act
articulates fiscal year 2027 Program expenditures.
This act also establishes the Vermont Rental Assistance Bridge Program within the
Vermont State Housing Authority to link households who require rental assistance to
permanent housing when the household does not have access to relevant U.S. Department
of Housing and Urban Development rental assistance.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on June 16, 2026
