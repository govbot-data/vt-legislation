House concurrent resolution recognizing May 2026 as National Foster Care Month in Vermont
Whereas, according to the U.S. Department of Education, at any given time, approximately 400,000 children
and youths are in foster care, and
Whereas, the foster parents in Vermont play a vital role in brightening and improving the lives of the
children and youth for whom they are responsible, and their role can be transformative, and
Whereas, on May 10, 1988, at the initiative of the U.S. Children’s Bureau, President Ronald Reagan signed
Presidential Proclamation 5820, establishing National Foster Care Month, shining a new light on foster care in
the United States and observing that “the family has the prime responsibility for nurturing children … [but]
some parents are unable to provide a minimally acceptable level of care for their children, and temporary or
permanent alternative placement is necessary … [and foster care provides] the opportunity to live in healthy
loving homes,” and
Whereas, on April 30, 2026, President Donald J. Trump signed Presidential Proclamation 11023 designating
May 2026 as this year’s National Foster Care Month, “celebrating the foster families whose devotion carries
our youngest citizens forward by helping them realize the full potential of their unique American Dream,” and
Whereas, “Engaging Youth. Building Supports. Strengthening Opportunities.” is the 2026 theme for
National Foster Care Month, which “highlights the need to create a child welfare system that authentically
engages and supports young people who are preparing to leave foster care,” now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly recognizes May 2026 as National Foster Care Month in Vermont, and be it
further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to the System of Care
Manager at the Department for Children and Families’ Family Services Division.
