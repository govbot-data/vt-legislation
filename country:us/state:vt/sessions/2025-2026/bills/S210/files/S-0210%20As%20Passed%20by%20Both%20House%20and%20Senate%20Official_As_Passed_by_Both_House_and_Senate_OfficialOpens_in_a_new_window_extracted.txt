10 An act relating to access to autopsy reports
11 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
12 Sec. 1. 18 V.S.A. § 505 is amended to read:
13 § 505. AUTOPSIES; AUTOPSY REPORTS
14 (a) When a Superior judge or the Attorney General orders an autopsy on
15 the body of a person, as provided in section 504 of this title, the Superior judge
16 or the Attorney General shall direct that such autopsy shall be made by the
17 Chief Medical Examiner, or under the Chief Medical Examiner’s direction,
18 unless, for good cause shown, such judge or the Attorney General otherwise
19 directs.

1 (b)(1) The Office of the Chief Medical Examiner shall disclose a
2 decedent’s autopsy report to the decedent’s personal representative in
3 compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of
4 1996, Pub. L. 104-191, and its associated regulations, including to the court-
5 appointed executor or administrator of the decedent’s estate and the decedent’s
6 heir at law, as determined by 14 V.S.A. chapter 42. Nothing in this
7 subdivision is intended to preclude disclosure of a decedent’s autopsy report to
8 other individuals as authorized by federal law.
9 (2) An individual who is not authorized to receive the autopsy report
10 pursuant to subdivision (1) of this subsection may petition the Probate
11 Division of the Superior Court for a copy of the autopsy report. The petition
12 shall contain an affidavit attesting to the petitioner’s relationship to the
13 decedent and the reason the petitioner is seeking the autopsy report. The
14 petitioner shall notify the Office of the Chief Medical Examiner within five
15 business days after filing the petition. If the Superior Court finds that the
16 petitioner has demonstrated a legitimate reason for the petitioner to obtain the
17 autopsy report, it shall order the Office of the Chief Medical Examiner to
18 provide a copy to the petitioner.

Division of the Superior Court for a copy of the autopsy report. The petition
shall contain an affidavit attesting to the petitioner’s relationship to the
decedent and the reason the petitioner is seeking the autopsy report. The
petitioner shall notify the Office of the Chief Medical Examiner and the State’s

Attorney of the county in which the death occurred within five days after filing
the petition. The Office and the State’s Attorney shall have an opportunity to
respond within 14 days after notice. If the Superior Court finds that the

(B) In determining good cause under subdivision (A) of this
subdivision (b)(2), the Superior Court shall consider:
(i) the relationship of the petitioner to the decedent and decedent’s
family;
(ii) whether the disclosure is necessary for the public evaluation of
governmental performance;
(iii) the seriousness of intrusion into the decedent and decedent
family’s privacy;
(iv) whether the disclosure is by the least intrusive means
available, including whether and to what degree redaction of some portions of
the autopsy report is appropriate; and
(v) the availability of similar information in other public records
regardless of form.
