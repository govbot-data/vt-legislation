Act No. 83 (S.210). An act relating to access to autopsy reports
Subjects: Health; Judiciary; Office of the Chief Medical Examiner; autopsy report
This act both codifies how disclosure of autopsy reports operates in Vermont under the
Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) and creates an
additional HIPAA-compliant path for disclosure of an autopsy report through the court
system. It allows an individual who is not otherwise authorized to obtain an autopsy
report under HIPAA to petition the Probate Division of the Superior Court for a copy of
the autopsy report. This act allows the court to order the Office of the Chief Medical
Examiner to provide a copy of the autopsy report, in whole or part, to the petitioner upon
a finding of good cause. In determining good cause, the act requires the court to consider
factors such as the relationship of the petitioner to the decedent and the decedent’s
family; the seriousness of the privacy invasion; and whether the disclosure interferes with
an ongoing criminal investigation.
Effective Date: July 1, 2026
