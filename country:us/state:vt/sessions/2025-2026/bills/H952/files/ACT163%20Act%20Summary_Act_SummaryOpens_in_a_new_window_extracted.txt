Act No. 163 (H.952). An act relating to capital construction and State bonding budget
adjustment
Subjects: Capital construction; State bonding; budget adjustment
This act adjusts the State’s biennial capital budget for fiscal years 2026 and 2027.
Bond-Funded Project Authorizations. This act increases bond-funded project
authorizations for the State’s biennial capital budget by $11,599,336.23, including the
following adjustments:
• a reduction of $1,616,586.82 for State Buildings
• an increase of $10,025,104.21 for Human Services
• an increase of $220,000.00 for Commerce and Community Development
• an increase of $1,250,000.00 for Veterans’ Home
• an increase of $1,720,818.84 for Judiciary
Reallocations and Reversions. This act increases reallocations of bonded dollars and
reversions of cash from prior capital appropriations by $10,177,891.89 to fund projects
authorized in this act.
Cash-Funded Project Authorizations. This act increases cash-funded project
authorizations for the State’s biennial capital budget by $23,418,454.70, reflecting new
spending authorizations in fiscal year 2027 for State Buildings, Human Services, Clean
Water Initiatives, Public Safety, Judiciary, and Vermont Historical Society from the
Capital Infrastructure subaccount of the Cash Fund for Capital and Essential Investments.
Department of Environmental Conservation. This act amends the chapter of Title 24
of the Vermont Statutes Annotated on the Special Environmental Revolving Fund to
authorize the Vermont Economic Development Authority to extend private loans
available to privately owned public water systems to eligible mobile home park water
systems.
Division for Historic Preservation. This act amends the section of Title 22 of the
Vermont Statutes Annotated on the acceptance of funds or gifts for historic sites and the
Vermont Archaeology Heritage Center to authorize the State Historic Preservation
Officer to solicit things of value on behalf of the Division for Historic Preservation with
the approval of the Secretary of Administration.
Department of Forests, Parks and Recreation. This act authorizes the Commissioner
of Forests, Parks and Recreation in fiscal year 2027 to enter into a long-term lease with
the Vermont Huts Association Ltd. for the use of a structure and land at Little River State
Park if certain conditions are met.
Department of Buildings and General Services. This act repeals and replaces earlier
authority for the Commissioner of Buildings and General Services to transfer to the Town

of Springfield a portion of the Southern State Correctional Facility property with new
time-limited authority to transfer a portion of the property for purposes of economic

Agency of Human Services. This act requires the Department for Children and
Families and the Department of Buildings and General Services to report on their plan to
develop the Green Mountain Youth Facility to the Joint Legislative Justice Oversight
Committee. This act also requires the Department of Buildings and General Services to
submit design documents and the Department for Children and Families to submit a draft
operating budget to the House Committees on Corrections and Institutions and on Human
Services and the Senate Committees on Institutions and on Health and Welfare prior to
formal approval.
Department of Corrections and Agency of Digital Services. This act requires the
Commissioner of Corrections and the State Chief Information Officer of Digital Services
to report regularly to the Joint Legislative Justice Oversight Committee on the plan for
providing network connectivity in State correctional facilities.
Stormwater Utilities. This act amends the chapters of Title 24 of the Vermont Statutes
Annotated on municipal and regional planning and development and on sewage, sewage
disposal, and stormwater systems to ensure that existing municipal authority to regulate
stormwater is not disrupted by the creation of a regional stormwater utility.
General Assembly. This act establishes a special committee consisting of the Joint
Legislative Management Committee and the Chairs of the House Committee on
Corrections and Institutions and the Senate Committee on Institutions to review the State
House entryway design at a regularly scheduled Joint Legislative Management
Committee meeting.
Effective Date: June 17, 2026
