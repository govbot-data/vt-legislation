2 An act relating to prohibiting the use and sale of the herbicide paraquat
3 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
4 Sec. 1. FINDINGS
5 The General Assembly finds that:
6 (1) Paraquat or paraquat dichloride is a synthetic, nonselective contact
7 herbicide that is a member of the bipyridylium chemical family and that
8 defoliates all vegetation that it touches.
9 (2) Paraquat was first registered in the United States in 1964, and, in
10 1978, the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) classified all paraquat
11 products as restricted use pesticides because of paraquat’s high acute toxicity
12 and potential for poisoning.
13 (3) Paraquat has been widely used throughout the United States since its
14 classification as a restricted use pesticide, and 24 currently registered pesticide
15 products approved for use in the United States contain paraquat.
16 (4) The Pesticide Action Network’s 2024 Consolidated List of Banned
17 Pesticides identifies 72 countries as banning the use of paraquat, including
18 Brazil, China, Malaysia, Peru, Saudi Arabia, South Korea, Turkey, and
19 Vietnam.
20 (5) The European Union banned paraquat in 2007 after a court
21 determined that the relevant regulatory body had failed to apply proper

1 procedures for evaluation of the herbicide and that the regulatory body was not
2 properly thorough in its assessments of paraquat’s effects on human and

4 (6) Multiple studies by the National Institutes of Health have
5 demonstrated that paraquat exposure substantially increases the risk of
6 Parkinson’s disease in those exposed to the herbicide.
7 (7) Studies by the National Institutes of Health and others have linked
8 exposure to paraquat to increased risk of non-Hodgkin lymphoma and
9 childhood leukemia.
10 (8) In 2021, as part of a product registration review of paraquat required
11 under the Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act (FIFRA), the
12 EPA issued an interim decision approving the continued registration of
13 paraquat for use in the United States, despite multiple public comments
14 objecting to approval and significant scientific evidence regarding the potential
15 health effects of paraquat exposure.
16 (9) Multiple parties subsequently moved to challenge the EPA interim
17 decision authorizing the continued registration and use of paraquat. In
18 particular, the challenging parties contested the EPA’s assessment of the
19 increased risk of Parkinson’s disease due to exposure to paraquat from
20 volatilization during use.

1 (10) In January 2025, after a petition from the EPA, a court allowed the
2 EPA to withdraw the interim registration approval for paraquat so that the EPA
3 may reconsider its initial conclusion concerning the potential for paraquat to
4 volatilize and to determine whether paraquat continues to meet FIFRA’s
5 registration standard.
6 (11) In October 2025, the EPA issued a memorandum titled
7 PARAQUAT: Review of the Volatilization Potential of Paraquat from Field
8 Uses and determined that there is a greater uncertainty regarding the potential
9 for paraquat to volatilize than previously understood. The EPA concluded that
10 more data is needed to understand the risk paraquat poses to bystanders.
11 (12) The EPA’s reevaluation of the risks of paraquat may take years,
12 and the EPA’s withdrawal of the interim registration and further evaluation
13 does not prohibit the continued use of paraquat during that review period.
14 (13) Instead of waiting for EPA action on whether or how to use
15 paraquat, the State of Vermont should ban the use of paraquat in order to
16 protect Vermonters from the known, significant health effects of exposure to
17 paraquat.
