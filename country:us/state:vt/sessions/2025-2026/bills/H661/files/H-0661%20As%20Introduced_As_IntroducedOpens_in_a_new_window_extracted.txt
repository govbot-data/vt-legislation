6 Subject: Labor; workers’ compensation; firefighters; cancer
7 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to add cancers of
8 the larynx, pharynx, and trachea to the cancers for which a firefighter may be
9 eligible to receive workers’ compensation. This bill also proposes to delete the
10 requirement that a firefighter be under 65 years of age for a cancer to be
11 presumed to have occurred in the line of duty.

5 (E) In the case of a firefighter, as defined in 20 V.S.A. § 3151(3) and

12 cancer screening evaluations as recommended by the American Cancer Society

14 while serving as a firefighter, and the evaluation indicated no evidence of
15 cancer; and
16 (II) the firefighter was engaged in firefighting duties or other
17 hazardous activities over a period of at least five years in Vermont prior to the
18 diagnosis; and
19 (III) the firefighter is under 65 years of age.
20 (ii) The presumption shall not apply to any firefighter who has

1 (iii) The disabling cancer shall be limited to leukemia, lymphoma,
2 or multiple myeloma, and cancers originating in the bladder, brain, breast,

5 (F) A firefighter who is diagnosed with cancer within 10 years of the
6 last active date of employment as a firefighter shall be eligible for benefits
7 under this subdivision. The date of injury shall be the date of the last injurious
8 exposure as a firefighter.
9 (G) It is recommended that fire departments:
