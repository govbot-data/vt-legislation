Act No. 167 (H.942). An act relating to miscellaneous agricultural subjects
Subjects: Water quality; agriculture; municipal and county government; Vermont
agricultural products; product grades, standards, and labeling; unit pricing
This act makes several miscellaneous changes to laws pertaining to agricultural
subjects. This act makes water quality training for farmers optional and makes changes
to requirements for managing and applying non-sewage waste on farms. This act also
makes several changes to price labeling for most retail establishments. Retail
establishments are required to list the unit price and total selling price for most consumer
commodities. Retail establishments are only permitted to change the price displayed on
an electronic shelf label when a retail establishment is closed.
This act requires the Commissioner of Taxes to study and provide recommendations
for including equine farming in the Use Value Appraisal Program. This act permits the
Rural Economic Development Initiative to support projects statewide for community
development initiatives that support agriculture, historic preservation, outdoor recreation,
and other critical economic development needs. This act also makes a technical
correction to the statute governing the Farm and Forestry Operations Security Special
Fund to clarify that it is the Secretary of Agriculture, Food and Markets, in consultation
with the Review Board, that makes award determinations.
Effective Date: July 1, 2026
