9 Subject: Labor; unemployment insurance; work search; overpayment of

11 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to require
12 individuals to repay any unemployment insurance benefit received during a
13 week in which the individual failed to conduct an interview for suitable work
14 or provided a prospective employer with false or inaccurate information that
15 prevented the employer from scheduling an interview with the individual. This
16 bill also proposes to strengthen the penalties for false statements made to
17 increase unemployment insurance payments. This bill also proposes to require
18 quarterly unemployment data cross-checks by the Department of Labor.

2 Sec. 1. 21 V.S.A. § 1347 is amended to read:
3 § 1347. NONDISCLOSURE OR MISREPRESENTATION;
4 OVERPAYMENTS; WAIVER
5 (a)(1) Any person who fails, without good cause, to make reasonable
6 efforts to secure suitable work when directed to do so by the employment
7 office or the Commissioner and has received any amount as benefits under this
8 chapter with respect to weeks for which the person is determined to be
9 ineligible because of the failure, and any person who by nondisclosure or
10 misrepresentation by the person, or by another, of a material fact (irrespective
11 of whether the nondisclosure or misrepresentation was known or fraudulent)
12 has received any amount as benefits under this chapter while any conditions
13 for the receipt of benefits imposed by this chapter were not fulfilled in the
14 person’s case or while the person was disqualified from receiving benefits,
15 shall be liable for the amount.
16 (2) A person shall be deemed to have failed to make reasonable efforts
17 to secure suitable work if the person:
18 (A) fails, without good cause, to conduct an interview for suitable
19 work after an employer has contacted the person to arrange such an interview;

10 Sec. 2. 21 V.S.A. § 1368 is amended to read:
11 § 1368. FALSE STATEMENTS TO INCREASE PAYMENTS
12 (a) A person shall not who intentionally make makes a false statement or
13 representation to obtain or, increase, or initiate any benefit or other payment
14 under this chapter, either for the employee whether for themselves or any other
15 person, shall, after notice and an opportunity for a hearing, be:
16 (1) liable to repay the amount of the overpaid benefits and any
17 applicable penalty imposed pursuant to section 1347 of this chapter;
18 (2) assessed a further administrative penalty of up to $5,000.00; and
19 (3) ineligible to receive benefits pursuant to this chapter for a period of
20 up to five years from the date on which the false statement or representation
21 was discovered.
