15 Subject: Commerce and trade; consumer protection; genetic data privacy
16 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to require direct-
17 to-consumer genetic testing companies and related providers to protect the
18 genetic data information of Vermonters. The bill requires companies to limit
19 data sharing, allows consumers to access their own data, and otherwise
20 provides other genetic data privacy protections to consumers.

6 § 2411. SHORT TITLE AND DEFINITIONS
7 (a) This subchapter shall be known, and may be cited, as the “Genetic
8 Information Privacy Act.”
9 (b) As used in this subchapter:
10 (1) “Affirmative authorization” means an action that demonstrates an
11 intentional decision by a consumer.
12 (2) “Biological sample” means any material part of the human,
13 discharge therefrom, or derivative thereof, such as tissue, blood, urine, or
14 saliva, known to contain deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA).
15 (3) “Consumer” means an individual who is a Vermont resident.
16 (4) “Dark pattern” means a user interface designed or manipulated with
17 the substantial effect of subverting or impairing user autonomy, decision
18 making, or choice.
19 (5) “Direct-to-consumer genetic testing company” means an entity that:
20 (A) sells, markets, interprets, or otherwise offers consumer-initiated
21 genetic testing products or services directly to consumers;

14 security, and integrity of a consumer’s genetic data, a direct-to-consumer
15 genetic testing company shall:
16 (1) provide clear and complete information regarding the company’s
17 policies and procedures for the collection, use, maintenance, and disclosure, as
18 applicable, of genetic data by making available to a consumer all of the
19 following:

4 (B) a prominent and easily accessible privacy notice that includes, at
5 a minimum, complete information about the company’s data collection,

12 § 2413. ENFORCEMENT
13 (a) A direct-to-consumer genetic testing company or service provider that
14 violates this subchapter or rules adopted pursuant to this subchapter commits
15 an unfair and deceptive act in commerce in violation of section 2453 of this
16 title.
17 (b) The Attorney General shall have the same authority under this
18 subchapter to make rules, conduct civil investigations, bring civil actions, and
19 enter into assurances of discontinuance against any person as provided under
20 chapter 63 of this title.
