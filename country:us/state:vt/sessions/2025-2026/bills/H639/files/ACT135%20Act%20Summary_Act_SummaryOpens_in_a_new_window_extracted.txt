Act No. 135 (H.639). An act relating to genetic data privacy
Subjects: Consumer protection; commerce and trade; genetic data privacy
This act provides privacy protections and rights for consumers for their genetic data.
Direct-to-consumer genetic testing companies are required to provide clear information to
consumers about the company’s policies on its collection and use of consumers’ genetic
data. Such companies must also obtain a consumer’s consent before the company uses
the genetic data or biological sample of the consumer beyond the primary purpose of the
genetic testing or service, including disclosing or transferring the genetic data or
biological sample to a third party.
The act requires that direct-to-consumer genetic testing companies implement
reasonable security procedures to protect against unauthorized access to consumers’
genetic data. It also gives consumers the right to request that their genetic data be
deleted. A violation of the act is enforced through the Vermont Consumer Protection
Act.
Effective Date: July 1, 2026
