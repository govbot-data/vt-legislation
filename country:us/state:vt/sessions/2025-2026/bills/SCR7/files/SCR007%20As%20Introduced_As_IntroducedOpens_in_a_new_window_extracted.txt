State of Vermont
Senate Chamber
Montpelier, Vermont
Senate Concurrent Resolution
Offered by Senators Heffernan, Baruth, Beck, Benson, Bongartz, Brennan, Brock,
Chittenden, Collamore, Cummings, Harrison, Hashim, Ingalls, Lyons, Major, Mattos, Morley,
Norris, Perchlik, Weeks, Westman and Williams,
S.C.R. 7. Senate concurrent resolution designating October 5, 2026, as Italian American Day
in Vermont.
Whereas, the proud history of the Italian American community in Vermont started in the
1880s, when economic conditions in Italy persuaded many Italians to emigrate from Italy to the
United States in hopes of a more prosperous future for their families, and
Whereas, Italian immigrants who were highly skilled in stone crafts settled in the Rutland-
Proctor area and found employment, especially at the Vermont Marble Company, a large
business that owned multiple quarries and became the source for the marble contained in
prominent public buildings across the country, and
Whereas, from 1890 to 1910, a significant population of new Italian immigrants, as well as
those who had originally settled in and around Rutland, came to Barre, to apply their artisan and
stone-cutting skills in the granite industry; they contributed significantly to the development of
Barre as the granite capital of the world, and their magnificent craftsmanship is on display in
Vermont and elsewhere, and
Whereas, Italian Americans were critical to the emergence of labor unions in Vermont, as
they endeavored to develop fair wages, benefits, and tolerable working conditions in the marble
and granite industries, and
Whereas, the contributions of Italian Americans are notable in many aspects of Vermont
society, including agriculture, the arts, civic participation, construction, economic development,
food production, manufacturing, and State and local government, and
Whereas, it is fitting to honor Italian Americans in recognition of their outstanding
contributions to the cultural and economic engine that is Vermont, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly designates October 5, 2026, as Italian American Day in Vermont,
and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to
Christine Stone.
