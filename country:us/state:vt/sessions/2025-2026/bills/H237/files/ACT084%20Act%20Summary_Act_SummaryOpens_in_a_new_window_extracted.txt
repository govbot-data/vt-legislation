Act No. 84 (H.237). An act relating to prescribing by doctoral-level psychologists
Subjects: Health; professional regulation; prescribing; doctoral-level psychologist
This act enables a psychologist to apply to the Board of Psychological Examiners for a
prescribing psychologist specialty when the individual holds a current license to practice
psychology at the doctoral level in the State; has completed a postdoctoral training
program in psychopharmacology; has completed clinical rotations in at least five practice
settings; has completed a certifying exam; and has met any other requirements
established in rule. This act requires a prescribing psychologist, prior to prescribing
medication, to enter into a collaborating agreement with a physician licensed to practice
medicine in Vermont with a specialty in psychiatry, who shall serve as a collaborating
practitioner. It further requires that the issuance of prescriptive authority by a
collaborating practitioner to a prescribing psychologist only include prescription drugs
for the treatment of mental health conditions that the collaborating practitioner generally
provides to patients in the normal course of practice. The act requires the Office of
Professional Regulation to submit a report to the General Assembly in November 2032
addressing the practice of prescribing by doctoral-level psychologists.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on July 1, 2026
