State of Vermont
Senate Chamber
Montpelier, Vermont
Senate Concurrent Resolution
Offered by Senators Lyons, Chittenden, and Ram Hinsdale,
Offered by Representatives Arsenault of Williston, Brady of Williston, and Burkhardt of
South Burlington,
S.C.R. 11. Senate concurrent resolution honoring the outstanding former Williston Fire Chief
Kenneth Morton Jr.,
Whereas, in 1982, the Town of Williston still retained a significant rural character with 11
farms, no buildings higher than two floors, no senior housing facilities, and absolutely no box
stores or large shopping centers, and, by 2020, the municipality was highly suburbanized, and
Whereas, to serve this community, which inevitably embarked on a transformative change
from semirural to overwhelmingly suburban, there existed a fire department consisting of an
exclusively volunteer force, and
Whereas, that year, Ken Morton, whose firefighting experience began a decade earlier as a
Norwich University freshman, joined the Williston Fire Department, which responded to 104
emergency calls during his inaugural year, and
Whereas, as Williston grew to a town of approximately 10,000 residents with all the suburban
attributes it lacked in 1982, the selectboard and the citizens supported a major metamorphosis in
the fire department’s organizational structure and services, and
Whereas, in 1992, the Town of Williston appointed Ken Morton as fire chief, and his great
leadership and guidance for the next 28 years propelled the department to new heights, and
Whereas, during former Chief Ken Morton’s tenure, the Williston Fire Department increased
its staff to 16 career and 16 on-call firefighters; in 2000, the department established an
emergency medical service, replacing the preexisting nonprofit organization; in 2010, the Town
of Williston expanded this service to include ambulance transport, and it now offers
paramedicine-level care; in 2007, a large modern new fire station opened and over the years the
vehicle fleet has been updated; and, by 2020, the department was responding to over 2,000
annual emergency calls, and
Whereas, Chief Ken Morton was widely respected among his professional peers across
Vermont and left an extraordinary legacy of firefighting excellence, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly honors the outstanding former Williston Fire Chief Kenneth
Morton Jr., and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Ken
Morton and the Williston Fire Department.
