7 Sec. 2. LEGISLATIVE INTENT; OVERDOSE PREVENTION CENTER
8 (a) It is the intent of the General Assembly to consider the operational
9 status and funding needs of Burlington’s overdose prevention center during the
10 fiscal year 2027 budget adjustment process. If the General Assembly finds that
11 a location for the overdose prevention center has been procured by lease or
12 purchase, the overdose prevention center is being fit up for its intended use,
13 and the overdose prevention center is currently or will imminently become
14 operational, it intends to appropriate up to $1,100,000.00 to the Department of
15 Health for the purpose of awarding a grant to the City of Burlington for the
16 work of the overdose prevention center.
17 (b) It is the intent of the General Assembly to continue to appropriate funds
18 annually from the Opioid Abatement Special Fund through at least fiscal year
19 2028 for the purpose of awarding grants to the City of Burlington for the
20 operation of the overdose prevention center, unless the Special Fund does not
21 have sufficient monies to fund this expenditure.

9 Sec. 7. APPROPRIATION REVIEW; FISCAL YEAR 2028 PROPOSAL
10 SUSPENSION
11 Notwithstanding 18 V.S.A. § 4772(e), the Opioid Settlement Advisory
12 Committee shall not accept funding proposals or make funding
13 recommendations from the Opioid Abatement Special Fund for fiscal year
14 2028, unless a program or initiative was previously identified in statute as
15 intended for annual funding. It instead shall review the outcomes of programs
16 and initiatives previously funded through the Opioid Abatement Special Fund
17 to assess effectiveness, long-term sustainability, and the appropriateness of the
18 Opioid Abatement Special Fund as a funding source, where applicable, to
19 inform recommendations made in fiscal year 2029 and thereafter.
