Act No. 141 (H.660). An act relating to fiscal year 2027 Opioid Abatement Special Fund
and Substance Misuse Prevention Special Fund appropriations
Subjects: Human services; opioid use disorder; Opioid Abatement Special Fund;
fiscal year 2027 appropriations
This act appropriates funds in fiscal year 2027 from the Opioid Abatement Special
Fund for programming, services, and staffing related to opioid use disorder, such as for
outreach or case management staff positions, peer recovery center coaches, and new
recovery residence beds. It specifies that it is the intent of the General Assembly to
consider the funding needs of the overdose prevention center during the fiscal year 2027
budget adjustment process and appropriate up to $1,100.000.00 if it finds that a location
for the overdose prevention center has been procured, the overdose prevention center is
being fit up for its intended use, and the overdose prevention center is currently or will
imminently become operational. This act reverts previous fiscal year Opioid Abatement
Special Fund appropriations for satellite locations, wound care, and stabilization beds
back to the Special Fund. It requires the Department of Health to submit quarterly
reports to the General Assembly regarding Opioid Abatement Special Fund expenditures
in fiscal year 2027.
This act amends the list of stakeholders that the Opioid Settlement Advisory
Committee is required to consult with to include the Health Equity Advisory
Commission. It specifies that each funding proposal submitted to the Advisory
Committee shall contain a sustainability plan from the applicant to ensure consideration
of future expenses and available resources apart from the Opioid Abatement Special
Fund. It further requires the Advisory Committee to consult with the Office of the
Attorney General regarding allowable uses of the Opioid Abatement Special Fund.
This act prohibits the Opioid Settlement Advisory Committee from accepting new
funding proposals from the Opioid Abatement Special Fund in fiscal year 2028, except
where previous statute anticipated annual funding. It instead requires the Advisory
Committee to review the outcomes of programs and initiatives previously funded through
the Opioid Abatement Special Fund to assess effectiveness, long-term sustainability, and
the appropriateness of the Special Fund as a funding source, where applicable, to inform
recommendations made in fiscal year 2029 and thereafter.
This act requires the Department of Health to submit a plan to the General Assembly
by December 15, 2026, containing recommendations for the implementation of one or
more syringe recovery models throughout the State, including requiring syringe service
providers to report on the percentage of distributed syringes that are returned to the
provider or otherwise collected.
Lastly, this act appropriates funds in fiscal year 2027 from the Substance Misuse
Prevention Special Fund for programming and services related to substance use disorder.
