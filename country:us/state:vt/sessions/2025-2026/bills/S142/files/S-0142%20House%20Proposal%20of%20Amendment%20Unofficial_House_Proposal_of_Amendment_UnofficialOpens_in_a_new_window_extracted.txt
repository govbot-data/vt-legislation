6 Sec. 1. PATHWAY TO LICENSURE FOR INTERNATIONALLY
7 TRAINED PHYSICIANS; REPORT
8 (a) On or before January 15, 2027, the Department of Health, in
9 collaboration with the Board of Medical Practice, shall provide to the House
10 Committees on Health Care and on Government Operations and Military
11 Affairs and the Senate Committees on Health and Welfare and on Government
12 Operations a report detailing a pathway to licensure for internationally trained
13 physicians. The report shall include the following information:
14 (1) a summary of other states’ processes for licensing internationally
15 trained physicians to practice medicine and, if available, data on the outcomes
16 of these processes and related programs;
17 (2) a description of the external resources needed to evaluate the
18 education, experience, and examinations of internationally trained physicians
19 and the availability of these resources;
20 (3) a proposal for licensing internationally trained physicians to practice
21 medicine in Vermont, including potential qualifications and supervision

4 Sec. 3. 26 V.S.A. § 1396 is added to read:
5 § 1396. PATHWAY TO LICENSURE FOR INTERNATIONALLY
6 TRAINED PHYSICIANS; RULEMAKING
7 Pursuant to the authority of the Commissioner in subsection 1351(e) of this
8 chapter, the Board shall adopt rules in accordance with 3 V.S.A. chapter 25 as
9 needed to enable the licensure of internationally trained physicians as set forth
10 in subsection 1391(g) of this chapter. The rules adopted by the Board shall
11 reflect the least restrictive form of regulation necessary to protect the public
12 interest and shall include:
13 (1) the qualifications necessary for an internationally trained physician
14 to obtain a provisional license to practice at a participating health care facility,
15 as defined in section 1397 of this chapter;
16 (2) the standards for participating health care facilities to use for the
17 evaluation and assessment of the holder of a provisional license; and
18 (3) the additional qualifications necessary for an internationally trained
19 physician to obtain a full license to practice medicine in this State following
20 successful completion of the provisional licensure period.

1 Sec. 4. 26 V.S.A. § 1397 is added to read:
2 § 1397. PARTICIPATING HEALTH CARE FACILITIES
3 (a) As used in this section:
4 (1) “Health care facility” means a hospital, federally qualified health
5 center, or community health center.
6 (2) “Participating health care facility” means a health care facility that
7 meets the requirements of this section and has the capacity to provide an
8 assessment and evaluation program designed in accordance with rules adopted
9 by the Board pursuant to section 1396 of this chapter to evaluate an
10 internationally trained physician holding a provisional license issued pursuant
11 to subdivision 1391(g)(1) of this chapter.

19 established by the Board by rule pursuant to section 1396 of this chapter;
20 (2) ensure that the mentoring, evaluation, assessment, and support of a
21 provisionally licensed physician is provided by one or more physicians who
