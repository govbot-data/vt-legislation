Act No. 172 (S.142). An act relating to a pathway to licensure for internationally trained
physicians
Subjects: Physicians; professions and occupations; health care professionals; Board
of Medical Practice; internationally trained physicians
This act establishes a process through which internationally trained physicians may
become licensed to practice medicine in Vermont. The act requires the Department of
Health, in collaboration with the Board of Medical Practice, to report to the General
Assembly by January 15, 2027, on a pathway to Vermont licensure for internationally
trained physicians, including providing information about other states’ processes for
licensing internationally trained physicians and the Department’s proposal for licensing
these physicians to practice medicine in Vermont. Beginning in 2028, the act allows the
Board of Medical Practice to issue provisional licenses to internationally trained
physicians to practice medicine at certain health care facilities in Vermont and allows the
Board to issue full licenses to internationally trained physicians who have successfully
completed the pathway to licensure in accordance with rules to be adopted by the Board.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on June 18, 2026
