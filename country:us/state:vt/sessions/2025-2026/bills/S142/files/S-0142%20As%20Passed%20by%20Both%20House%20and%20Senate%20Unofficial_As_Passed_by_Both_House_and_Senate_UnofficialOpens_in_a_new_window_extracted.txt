5 Sec. 1. PATHWAY TO LICENSURE FOR INTERNATIONALLY
6 TRAINED PHYSICIANS; REPORT
7 (a) On or before January 15, 2027, the Department of Health, in
8 collaboration with the Board of Medical Practice, shall provide to the House
9 Committees on Health Care and on Government Operations and Military
10 Affairs and the Senate Committees on Health and Welfare and on Government
11 Operations a report detailing a pathway to licensure for internationally trained
12 physicians. The report shall include the following information:
13 (1) a summary of other states’ processes for licensing internationally
14 trained physicians to practice medicine and, if available, data on the outcomes
15 of these processes and related programs;
16 (2) a description of the external resources needed to evaluate the
17 education, experience, and examinations of internationally trained physicians
18 and the availability of these resources;
19 (3) a proposal for licensing internationally trained physicians to practice
20 medicine in Vermont, including potential qualifications and supervision
21 requirements for licensure, proposed requirements for recency of practice, a

4 Sec. 3. 26 V.S.A. § 1396 is added to read:
5 § 1396. PATHWAY TO LICENSURE FOR INTERNATIONALLY
6 TRAINED PHYSICIANS; RULEMAKING
7 Pursuant to the authority of the Commissioner in subsection 1351(e) of this
8 chapter, the Board shall adopt rules in accordance with 3 V.S.A. chapter 25 as
9 needed to enable the licensure of internationally trained physicians as set forth
10 in subsection 1391(g) of this chapter. The rules adopted by the Board shall
11 reflect the least restrictive form of regulation necessary to protect the public
12 interest and shall include:
13 (1) the qualifications necessary for an internationally trained physician
14 to obtain a provisional license to practice at a participating health care facility,
15 as defined in section 1397 of this chapter;
16 (2) the standards for participating health care facilities to use for the
17 evaluation and assessment of the holder of a provisional license; and
18 (3) the additional qualifications necessary for an internationally trained
19 physician to obtain a full license to practice medicine in this State following
20 successful completion of the provisional licensure period.

2 § 1397. PARTICIPATING HEALTH CARE FACILITIES
3 (a) As used in this section:
