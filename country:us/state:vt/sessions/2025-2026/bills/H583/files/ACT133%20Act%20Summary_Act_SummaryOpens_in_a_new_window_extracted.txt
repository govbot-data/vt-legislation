Act No. 133 (H.583). An act relating to clinical decision making
Subjects: Health; health care facilities; clinical decision making; corporate practice of
medicine
This act establishes limitations on private equity groups’ and hedge funds’ control over clinical
decision making. It prohibits a private equity group or hedge fund that is involved with a health
care facility in Vermont from interfering with health care providers’ judgment in making health
care decisions and from exercising control over many aspects of patient care, including setting
prices for a health care provider’s services, making decisions about the coding and billing of
diagnoses and procedures, and selecting the medical equipment and supplies. The act allows a
health care provider who is aggrieved by the actions of a private equity group or hedge fund in
violation of the act to bring an action in Superior Court.
The act requires all health care facilities and management services organizations to report to the
Green Mountain Care Board by March 1, 2027, regarding whether any private equity group or
hedge fund held an ownership or investment interest in them as of June 1, 2026. The act requires
those that did have private equity group or hedge fund ownership or investment as of June 1, 2026,
to provide to the Board information about their financial interests, an organizational chart, and the
most recent year’s profit and loss statement and balance sheet. After March 1, 2027, the act
requires a health care facility or management services organization to provide information to the
Board any time it engages in new or modified involvement with a private equity group or hedge
fund. There are exemptions from reporting for nursing homes, health care staffing companies,
federally qualified health centers, and entities that offer only telehealth services in Vermont. Most
of the information will be public except that an individual health care provider’s Social Security
number and personal contact information and the profit and loss statements and balance sheets are
exempt from the Public Records Act and must be kept confidential. The act provides civil
penalties for knowingly failing to report and for making material misrepresentations in a report.
The act requires the Green Mountain Care Board to post a report on its website by July 1, 2027,
and every two years thereafter about the information reported by health care facilities and
management services organizations that have ownership or investment involvement from private
equity groups and hedge funds. The act also allows the Board to share information reported to it
with the Attorney General, the Secretary of State, and other State agencies and officials to avoid
duplicative reporting requirements and to facilitate appropriate oversight and enforcement.
Effective Date: July 1, 2026
