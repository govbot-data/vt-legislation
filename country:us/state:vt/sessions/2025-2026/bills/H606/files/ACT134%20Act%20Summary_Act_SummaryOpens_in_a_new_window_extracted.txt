Act No. 134 (H.606). An act relating to firearms relinquishment and storage procedures
Subjects: Firearms; criminal and civil procedures
This act enacts the recommendations of the Firearm Surrender Order Compliance
Working Group, which the General Assembly established last year to develop a uniform
process to ensure compliance with court orders to relinquish or surrender firearms. The
act permits relinquishment to a third party other than a law enforcement agency or a
firearms dealer only if the court first holds a hearing and finds that third-party
relinquishment adequately protects the safety of the parties. Third parties must agree to
undergo a firearms background check through the National Instant Criminal Background
Check System (NICS) and must execute an affidavit acknowledging receipt of the
firearms and assuming responsibility for them. The act details the procedures for law
enforcement storage with firearms dealers; sets forth the procedures for release and sale
of relinquished firearms, including permitting the agency or dealer to retain all proceeds
from the sale; and provides participating firearms dealers with the same immunity from
civil or criminal liability for storing firearms that law enforcement agencies have under
current law. The act also requires the Department of Public Safety to direct the Law
Enforcement Advisory Board (LEAB) to adopt a statewide model law enforcement
policy addressing firearms access by persons who are prohibited from possessing
firearms, and provides that State and local law enforcement agencies that do not adopt
their own policies by June 30, 2027, are deemed to have adopted the LEAB model policy.
Effective Date: July 1, 2026
