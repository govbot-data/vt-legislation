1 (1) Vermont has a compelling interest in protecting its residents,

17 coexist.

1 (a) State policy. It is the policy of the State of Vermont that all Vermont
2 educational institutions provide safe, orderly, civil, and positive learning
3 environments. Harassment, hazing, and bullying have no place and will not be
4 tolerated in Vermont schools. No Vermont student should feel threatened or
5 be discriminated against while enrolled in a Vermont school.
6 (b) Prevention policies. Each school board shall develop, adopt, ensure the
7 enforcement of, and make available in the manner described under subdivision
8 563(1) of this title harassment, hazing, and bullying prevention policies that
9 shall be at least as stringent as model policies developed by the Secretary. Any
10 school board that fails to adopt one or more of these policies shall be presumed
11 to have adopted the most current model policy or policies published by the
12 Secretary.
13 (c) Notice. Annually, prior to the commencement of curricular and
14 cocurricular activities, the school board shall provide notice of the policy and
15 procedures developed under this subchapter to students, custodial parents or
16 guardians of students, and staff members, including reference to the
17 consequences of misbehavior contained in the plan required by section 1161a
18 of this title. Notice to students shall be in age-appropriate language and should
19 include examples of harassment, hazing, and bullying. At a minimum, this
20 notice shall appear in any publication that sets forth the comprehensive rules,
21 procedures, and standards of conduct for the school. The school board shall

6 Sec. 3. 16 V.S.A. § 914 is added to read:
7 § 914. FREEDOM OF EXPRESSION
8 (a) The General Assembly finds that in order to encourage students to
9 become educated, informed, and responsible members of society, instruction
10 on the First Amendment to the U.S. Constitution and Chapter I, Article 13 of
11 the Vermont Constitution shall be provided in public schools.
12 (b) The Agency of Education shall develop a freedom of expression
13 curriculum for public elementary and secondary schools that provides students
14 with age-appropriate foundational knowledge of the protections offered by the
15 First Amendment to the U.S. Constitution and Chapter I, Article 13 of the
16 Vermont Constitution that enables students to distinguish between political
17 advocacy and bias-based harassment such as antisemitism, Islamophobia, and
18 other forms of religious bias.
19 Sec. 4. CURRICULUM IMPLEMENTATION
20 On or before January 1, 2027, the Agency of Education shall develop the
21 freedom of expression curriculum required pursuant to Sec. 3 of this act, and
