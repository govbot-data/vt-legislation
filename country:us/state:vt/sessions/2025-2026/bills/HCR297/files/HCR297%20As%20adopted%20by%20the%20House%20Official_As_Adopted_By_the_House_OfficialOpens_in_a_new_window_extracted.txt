House concurrent resolution honoring Vermont Fish and Wildlife Department Director of Fisheries Eric
Palmer and extending future best wishes
Whereas, University of Minnesota–educated biologist Eric Palmer has epitomized leadership excellence at
the Vermont Fish and Wildlife Department (DFW) through significant contributions to the improvement of
fisheries management in Vermont and the Northeast and in his role as a superb supervisor who encourages staff
innovation and personally fulfilling professional advancement, and
Whereas, Eric Palmer began his DFW career on January 10, 1994, as a fisheries biologist, and, in 2000, a
superb job performance and demonstrated leadership ability led to his appointment as DFW’s Director of
Fisheries, and
Whereas, as Director of Fisheries, Eric Palmer has centered his personnel focus on collaboration and
empowering and supporting his staff to pursue individual strengths and interests to foster meaningful careers,
while advancing DFW’s mission and objectives, and
Whereas, not only does the staff appreciate Eric Palmer’s leadership, but his cooperative and team-based
managerial approach has resulted in successful conservation projects, including enabling the positive outcome
of a lake trout restoration initiative in Lake Champlain, fostering the effectiveness of the Connecticut River
Atlantic Salmon Commission, developing great results with a strategic wood addition habitat project, and
creating a strong and mutually advantageous system of angler communication and outreach, and
Whereas, in 2026, Eric Palmer, among whose personal milestones as DFW’s Director of Fisheries was his
election and outstanding service as president of the Atlantic International Chapter of the American Fisheries
Society, is concluding his stellar tenure at DFW, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly honors Vermont Fish and Wildlife Department Director of Fisheries Eric Palmer
and extends future best wishes, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Eric Palmer.
