11 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to require the
12 Division of Emergency Management to develop an emergency operations plan
13 (EOP) for each dam with a population at risk of 100 or more persons in order
14 to prepare emergency responders in the area of potential inundation from the
15 dam. The bill also would clarify that the Governor or the Director of
16 Emergency Management without municipal approval may require the
17 evacuation of an area subject to inundation from a dam failure when there is
18 dam failure or an imminent risk of failure. In addition, the bill would require
19 the Division of Emergency Management to conduct a pilot project under
20 which the Division develops EOPs for two State-owned high-hazard potential
21 dams with populations at risk of 1,000 or more persons.

3 Sec. 1. 20 V.S.A. § 52 is added to read:
4 § 52. DAM SAFETY; EMERGENCY OPERATIONS PLANS; TRAINING
5 (a) If the Department of Environmental Conservation has classified a dam
6 as a high-hazard potential dam, the Division of Emergency Management in
7 coordination with the Department of Environmental Conservation shall
8 complete or fund the completion of an emergency operations plan (EOP) for
9 the dam according to the requirements of this section. The Division of
10 Emergency Management shall complete an EOP required under this section:
11 (1) on or before January 15, 2030, if a dam has a population at risk of
12 1,000 or more persons; or
13 (2) on or before January 15, 2031, if a dam has a population at risk of
14 100 or more but fewer than 1,000 persons.
15 (b)(1) In order to ensure the sufficiency of an EOP required under this
16 section to protect public lives and property, the Division of Emergency
17 Management shall coordinate with the owner of the dam regarding the
18 substance and application of the required EOP. The coordination by the
19 Division shall include coordinating with and collecting input from municipal
20 officials, emergency responders, regional planning commissions, and other

Sec. 2. STATE OF VERMONT EMERGENCY OPERATIONS PLANNING
PILOT PROJECT; REPORT
(a)(1) The Division of Emergency Management, in coordination with the
Department of Environmental Conservation, shall conduct a pilot project
under which the Division shall develop a set of emergency operations plans
(EOPs) for two State-owned dams that have been classified as high-hazard
potential. One of the dams shall have a population at risk of 1,000 or more
persons and the other shall have a population at risk of 100 or more but fewer
than 1,000 persons.

(2) The set of EOPs for each dam shall include an EOP for each
municipality in the inundation zone of the dam.
(b)(1) In preparing the EOPs required under subsection (a) of this section
and in order to ensure the sufficiency of the EOPs to protect public lives and
property, the Division shall coordinate with and collect input from those
entities traditionally involved in regional and municipal emergency
management, including municipal officials; emergency responders; regional
planning commissions; State and regional search and rescue partners,
including swiftwater rescue providers; and other relevant interested parties
located in the regional area that would be inundated if the dam were to fail.
The Division also shall coordinate with any owner or operator of a
hydroelectric generation facility located at a State-owned dam.
(2) The Division of Emergency Management may hire a contractor,
including a regional planning commission, to complete the requirements of this
section, including one or both of the EOPs required under subsection (a) of
this section.
(c) Each EOP required to be completed under subsection (a) of this section
shall:
(1) be coordinated with each dam’s emergency action plan and shall
utilize each dam’s emergency action plan inundation maps;
