Act No. 147 (S.193). An act relating to establishing a forensic facility for certain criminal
justice-involved persons
Subjects: Criminal procedure; public safety; mental health; forensic facility;
competency restoration
This act requires the Agency of Human Services to establish a forensic facility to
house and treat certain persons who need treatment after having been charged with or
convicted of certain crimes. A person would be placed in the facility if the person is
found incompetent to stand trial for or not guilty by reason of insanity of a criminal
offense punishable by a sentence of life in prison. The person receives competency
restoration or other treatment services while at the facility and is periodically reevaluated,
and the person may be released under supervision if the person is no longer dangerous or
cannot be restored to competency. Until July 1, 2029, the services are provided at a
correctional facility through an interim forensic and competency restoration program
established through emergency rules adopted by the Agency of Human Services. The
emergency rules are repealed on July 1, 2029, at which time the permanent forensic
facility becomes effective and its operation becomes distinct from a correctional facility
setting. Prior to that date, the act requires the Secretary to provide status reports to the
Joint Justice Oversight Committee and to provide the General Assembly with a feasibility
plan for the development and operation of the facility.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on July 1, 2026
