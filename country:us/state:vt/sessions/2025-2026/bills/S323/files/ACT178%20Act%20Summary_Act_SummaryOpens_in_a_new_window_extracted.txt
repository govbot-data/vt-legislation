Act No. 178 (S.323). An act relating to miscellaneous agricultural subjects
Subjects: Agriculture; pesticides; hemp oversight; Concentrated Animal Feeding
Operation Permit Program Working Group; miscellaneous subjects
This act makes several miscellaneous changes to laws pertaining to agricultural
subjects. This act includes a technical correction regarding a milk producer’s request for
a hearing and enables the Farm-to-School Program to award contracts in addition to
grants. This act repeals the interstate Pest Control Compact that has not been active since
approximately 2014, makes changes to pesticide licenses and applicator examination
requirements, and makes technical changes to seed law.
This act also consolidates the Vermont Agricultural Credit Program (VACP) within
the Vermont Economic Development Authority (VEDA). VACP is currently
administered by the Vermont Agricultural Credit Corporation that is administered by
VEDA; this act dissolves the Vermont Agricultural Credit Corporation, and VEDA will
administer VACP directly. This act makes technical and conforming changes but does
not substantively alter VACP.
This act transitions oversight of hemp growing and processing, hemp products, and
enforcement from the Agency of Agriculture, Food and Markets to the Cannabis Control
Board. These sections largely preserve current law, make technical changes, and make
some substantive changes to conform to evolving federal law that would redefine
tetrahydrocannabinol concentration limits for hemp. The Cannabis Control Board will
require annual licensure and fees for hemp producers, processors, and certain hemp
products. Any person who causes unregistered hemp or unregistered cannabis products
to be shipped to anyone other than a licensed cannabis laboratory in the State could be
subject to criminal and civil penalties.
This act permits the Natural Resources Conservation Council to obtain mortgages to
purchase property. The act also creates a Concentrated Animal Feeding Operation
(CAFO) Permit Program Working Group to make recommendations for CAFO
permitting. The act requires the Secretary of Natural Resources to hire a third-party
consultant to provide training on CAFO permitting, but this requirement is contingent on
a future appropriation for fiscal year 2027. This act also makes a technical correction to
the statute governing the Farm and Forestry Operations Security Special Fund to clarify
that it is the Secretary of Agriculture, Food and Markets, in consultation with the Review
Board, that makes award determinations.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on June 18, 2026
