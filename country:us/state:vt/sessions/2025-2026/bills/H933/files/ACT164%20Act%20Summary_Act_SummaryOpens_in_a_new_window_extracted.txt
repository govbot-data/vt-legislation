Act No. 164 (H.933). An act relating to miscellaneous administrative and policy changes to
the tax laws
Subjects: Income tax; taxation; Education Fund; use value appraisal; property tax;
property transfer tax; estate tax; meals and rooms tax; transportation fund;
purchase and use tax; General Fund; property tax credit; education finance;
property valuation; homestead declaration; municipal grand list stabilization;
equalization study; health IT fund; inflation index; federal tax credit for
contributions to scholarship granting organizations; grand lists; grand list
assessment date; municipal tax collection
This act makes several technical, administrative, and policy changes to Vermont tax
laws and fees.
This act creates a process for Vermont taxpayers to donate to scholarship granting
organizations and receive a tax credit on their federal income tax returns. It creates
Vermont-specific requirements for qualifying scholarship granting organizations and the
educational programs for which they provide scholarships.
This act amends the statutory fee-setting authority for access to Department of Fish
and Wildlife properties. The Department is allowed to set fees for certain research and
commercial uses and to set the tuition for Green Mountain Conservation Camps through
regulation. All other land access fees are to be set through statute.
Starting on July 1, 2031, this act changes the grand list assessment date for annual
property valuation from April 1 to January 1.
This act decouples Vermont from federal income tax relating to bonus depreciation for
qualified production property, certain federal deductions for domestic research and
experimental procedures, certain deductions for foreign derived income, and income
excluded from the disposition of qualified small business stock. It also adds clarifying
language regarding the treatment and tax administration relating to depreciating property
for which Vermont disallows bonus depreciation. This act further links Vermont income
tax up to the federal income tax for items that Vermont has not expressly decoupled.
This act increases the amount of tax credit available for research and development
conducted in this State.
This act increases the amount of tax credits that may be awarded annually for the
Downtown and Village Center Tax Credit.
This act changes the allocations of tax revenue from meals and rooms and purchase
and use taxes.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on January 1, 2025
