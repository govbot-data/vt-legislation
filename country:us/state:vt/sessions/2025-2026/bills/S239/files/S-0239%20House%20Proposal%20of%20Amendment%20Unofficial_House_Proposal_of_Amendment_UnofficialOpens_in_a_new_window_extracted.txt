2 An act relating to the Child Abuse and Neglect Reporting Working Group
3 The House proposes to the Senate to amend the bill by striking out all after
4 the enacting clause and inserting in lieu thereof the following:
5 Sec. 1. CHILD ABUSE AND NEGLECT REPORTING WORKING
6 GROUP; REPORT
7 (a) The General Assembly finds:
8 (1) According to Child Trends, a research organization focused on
9 improving the lives of children, youth, and families, data shows that from 2022
10 through 2024 Vermont had a rate of referrals to child welfare services that was
11 over three times higher than the national level, with a rate of referral of 166 per
12 1,000 children in Vermont compared to 50 per 1,000 children nationally.
13 Additionally, only 17 percent of such referrals in Vermont met the criteria for
14 further action via an assessment or investigation compared to 54 percent
15 nationally.
16 (2) While the General Assembly recently reviewed and revised child
17 abuse and neglect substantiation procedures that occur after a referral has been
18 accepted by the Department for Children and Families, there has not been a
19 similar review of the training and requirements for mandatory reporting of
20 suspected child abuse or neglect to ensure they employ best practices and
21 provide sufficient guidance and resources for mandatory reporters.

1 (3) Data from Child Trends further shows that post-response services
2 such as mental health services, substance misuse treatment, family therapy,
3 child care, parenting education, and resources to assist families living in
4 poverty were provided to only 28 percent of victims in Vermont compared
5 with the national average of 57 percent.
6 (4) The provision of services to children and families prior to, during,
7 and after a report of suspected child abuse or neglect is an essential element in
8 a comprehensive child protection system.
9 (b) There is created the Child Abuse and Neglect Reporting Working
10 Group for the purpose of examining the existing statutes and the Department
11 for Children and Families’ rules and policies regarding mandatory reporting of
12 abuse and neglect of a child and recommending changes to modernize them
13 and reflect current best practices. During its examination of mandatory
14 reporting, the Working Group shall consider what services and strategies may
15 be employed prior to any report of suspected abuse or neglect for the purpose
16 of providing assistance to families before a situation rises to the level of
17 requiring a report.
18 (c) The Working Group shall be composed of the following members:
19 (1) a member with lived experience as an abused or neglected child,
20 appointed by the Vermont Child, Youth, and Family Advisory Council;

4 (f)(1) In developing its recommendations, the Working Group shall
5 prioritize issues related to:
6 (A) providing clarity regarding statutory definitions applicable to
7 mandatory reporters;
8 (B) establishing consistency between statutory requirements and

10 (C) identifying practical implementation challenges faced by
11 mandatory reporters in complying with existing law;
12 (D) assessing the appropriateness and efficacy of provisions in
13 33 V.S.A. §§ 4912 and 4913 regarding the definitions applicable to mandatory
14 reporters, who should be a mandatory reporter, the process for mandatory
15 reporting, the penalties for failure to report, and any exemptions from the
16 reporting requirement; and
17 (E) identifying alternatives to reporting suspected child abuse or
18 neglect when such alternatives are in the best interests of the child.
19 (2) The Working Group shall avoid expanding its review into matters
20 unrelated to mandatory reporting obligations, thresholds, or processes unless
