4 Sec. 1. CHILD ABUSE AND NEGLECT REPORTING WORKING
5 GROUP; REPORT
6 (a) The General Assembly finds:
7 (1) According to Child Trends, a research organization focused on
8 improving the lives of children, youth, and families, data shows that from 2022
9 through 2024 Vermont had a rate of referrals to child welfare services that was
10 over three times higher than the national level, with a rate of referral of 166 per
11 1,000 children in Vermont compared to 50 per 1,000 children nationally.
12 Additionally, only 17 percent of such referrals in Vermont met the criteria for
13 further action via an assessment or investigation compared to 54 percent
14 nationally.
15 (2) While the General Assembly recently reviewed and revised child
16 abuse and neglect substantiation procedures that occur after a referral has been
17 accepted by the Department for Children and Families, there has not been a
18 similar review of the training and requirements for mandatory reporting of
19 suspected child abuse or neglect to ensure they employ best practices and
20 provide sufficient guidance and resources for mandatory reporters.
21 (3) Data from Child Trends further shows that post-response services
22 such as mental health services, substance misuse treatment, family therapy,

4 (4) The provision of services to children and families prior to, during,

6 a comprehensive child protection system.
7 (b) There is created the Child Abuse and Neglect Reporting Working
8 Group for the purpose of examining the existing statutes and the Department
9 for Children and Families’ rules and policies regarding mandatory reporting of
10 abuse and neglect of a child and recommending changes to modernize them
11 and reflect current best practices. During its examination of mandatory
12 reporting, the Working Group shall consider what services and strategies may
13 be employed prior to any report of suspected abuse or neglect for the purpose
14 of providing assistance to families before a situation rises to the level of
15 requiring a report.
16 (c) The Working Group shall be composed of the following members:
17 (1) a member with lived experience as an abused or neglected child,
18 appointed by the Vermont Child, Youth, and Family Advisory Council;
19 (2) a member with lived experience as an individual who was reported
20 for suspected child abuse or neglect and an investigation found the report to be
21 unsubstantiated, appointed by the Vermont Parent Representation Center;

1 (f)(1) In developing its recommendations, the Working Group shall
2 prioritize issues related to:
3 (A) providing clarity regarding statutory definitions applicable to
4 mandatory reporters;
5 (B) establishing consistency between statutory requirements and
6 Department for Children and Families rules, guidance, and training materials;
7 (C) identifying practical implementation challenges faced by
8 mandatory reporters in complying with existing law;
9 (D) assessing the appropriateness and efficacy of provisions in
10 33 V.S.A. §§ 4912 and 4913 regarding the definitions applicable to mandatory
11 reporters, who should be a mandatory reporter, the process for mandatory
12 reporting, the penalties for failure to report, and any exemptions from the
13 reporting requirement; and
14 (E) identifying alternatives to reporting suspected child abuse or
15 neglect when such alternatives are in the best interests of the child.
16 (2) The Working Group shall avoid expanding its review into matters
17 unrelated to mandatory reporting obligations, thresholds, or processes unless
18 necessary to resolve an identified reporting issue.
