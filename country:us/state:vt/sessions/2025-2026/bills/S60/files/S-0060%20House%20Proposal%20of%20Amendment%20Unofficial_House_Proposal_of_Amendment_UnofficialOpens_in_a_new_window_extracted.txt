4 The House proposes to the Senate to amend the bill by striking out all after

6 Sec. 1. FINDINGS
7 The General Assembly finds that:
8 (1) In 2023 and 2024, Vermont experienced extreme flooding and other
9 weather-based disasters that devastated farms and other working lands
10 businesses across the State.
11 (2) Many existing State and federal programs that are designed to
12 support farms and forestry operations are difficult to access, are
13 administratively burdensome, are not equitably distributed to small- and
14 medium-scale farmers and forestry operations, and currently do not meet the
15 needs of Vermont farmers and forestry operations in a holistic way.
16 (3) In particular, because federal crop insurance programs are not
17 designed to serve the needs of smaller scale or more diversified farming
18 operations, many Vermont farmers are not covered by crop insurance.

19 or otherwise uncovered losses due to eligible weather conditions, up to a
20 maximum annual award of five percent of the undesignated and unreserved
21 funds in the Farm and Forestry Operations Security Special Fund at the
