Act No. 169 (H.949). An act relating to homestead property tax yields, the nonhomestead
property tax rate, and technical changes to education finance
Subjects: Education; taxation and finance; education property tax; special
education; City of Barre; nonhomestead rate; education finance; overpayment; tax
increment financing district; homestead yields; homestead property tax credit and
renter credit; census grant; statewide adjustment
This act sets the property dollar equivalent yield at $9,401.00 and the income dollar
equivalent yield at $12,960.00 for the purpose of setting homestead property tax rates in
fiscal year 2027. This act sets the nonhomestead property tax rate in fiscal year 2027 at
$1.643 per $100.00 of equalized education property value.
This act makes a technical correction to the application of the statewide adjustment for
determining property tax credits. This act also appropriates funds from the Education
Fund to refund the City of Barre for overpayments from the City’s TIF district. This act
additionally codifies the fiscal year 2027 uniform base amount for purposes of calculating
census grants to support special education and adds a perpetual inflator.
This act extends the existing exemption from the excess spending penalty for voter-
approved bond payments to bonds approved by voters prior to July 1, 2026. This act also
reduces the excess spending threshold in fiscal year 2028 from 118 percent to 115.5
percent of the excess spending base and annually reduces the excess spending threshold
until reaching 112.5 percent of the excess spending base in fiscal year 2032.
This act expands the renter credit for claim year 2027 only by increasing the statutory
cap on the credit from $2,500.00 to $3,250.00 and increasing the factor for determining
the maximum credit from 10 percent of fair market rent to 12.5 percent of fair market
rent. This act also permanently expands income sensitivity measures under the property
tax credit system beginning in fiscal year 2028 by increasing the maximum income
threshold from $47,000.00 to $50,000.00 for both the statewide and municipal circuit
breakers.
This act additionally makes conforming changes to the excess spending penalty and
income sensitivity measures to account for the rollout of the foundation formula in fiscal
year 2030.
This act limits increases in tuition paid to a public or approved independent receiving
school for fiscal years 2028 through 2032 to the annual growth in statewide education
spending.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on July 1, 2026
