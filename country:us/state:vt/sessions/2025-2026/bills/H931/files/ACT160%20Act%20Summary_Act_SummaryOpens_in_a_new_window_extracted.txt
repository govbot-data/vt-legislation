Act No. 160 (H.931). An act relating to miscellaneous changes in education law
Subjects: Education; moratorium on approved independent schools; therapeutic
approved independent schools; Interstate Compact for Education; Agency of
Education background checks
This act makes several miscellaneous changes to Vermont education law. First, it
creates an exception to the moratorium on approval of initial applications for new
approved independent schools for currently approved therapeutic approved independent
schools undergoing a change of ownership that would require a new initial application to
be submitted to the State Board of Education. This act requires such applications to be
processed according to applicable State and federal law as long as the school will remain
a therapeutic approved independent school after the change in ownership is complete.
Second, this act codifies Vermont’s membership in the Intestate Compact for
Education.
Third, this act requires the Agency of Education to request criminal record information
for any person the Agency is prepared to recommend for any full-time, part-time,
temporary, or contract position with the Agency if such person will have or has the
potential to have unsupervised contact with students.
Fourth, this act makes several changes to the Intercollegiate Sexual Harm Prevention
Council, including changing membership composition, eliminating several Council
duties, eliminating the assistance of the Network Against Domestic and Sexual Violence
and replacing it with the administrative support of the Council’s co-chairs, eliminating
the Council’s annual reporting requirement to the General Assembly, requiring co-chairs
instead of one chair, requiring meeting twice per year instead of quarterly, and
eliminating member entitlement to per diem compensation and reimbursement of
expenses.
Fifth, this act makes several changes to the Harassment, Hazing, and Bullying
Prevention Advisory Council, including adding several new members, providing more
specificity to the Council’s duties, requiring an annual report to the General Assembly,
and adding member entitlement to per diem compensation and reimbursement for not
more than 12 meetings per year. This act includes an appropriation of $21,000.00 from
the General Fund to the Agency of Education in fiscal year 2027 for member per diem
compensation and reimbursement of expenses.
Finally, this act amends the definition of “cost-saving measure” in the energy
performance contracting statute to include additions.
Effective Date: July 1, 2026
