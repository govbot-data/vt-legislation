5 Subject: Criminal procedures; innocence protection; compensation for
6 wrongful convictions
7 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to clarify that a
8 person who is convicted and imprisoned for a crime, and subsequently
9 exonerated after being found innocent of the crime, is entitled to compensation
10 for the wrongful conviction irrespective of whether the person was exonerated
11 because of DNA testing or for other reasons.
12 An act relating to compensation for wrongful convictions
13 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
14 Sec. 1. 13 V.S.A. chapter 182, subchapter 2 is amended to read:
15 Subchapter 2. Compensation for Wrongful Convictions
16 § 5572. RIGHT OF ACTION; PROCEDURE
17 (a) A person convicted and imprisoned for a crime of which the person was
18 exonerated pursuant to this chapter shall have a cause of action for damages
19 against the State.

2 § 5576. LIMITATIONS
3 (a) Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, an action for
4 compensation under this subchapter shall be commenced within three years
5 after the person is exonerated pursuant to subchapter 1 of this chapter as
6 defined in subdivisions 5572(b)(1)–(4) of this title through the person’s
7 conviction being reversed or vacated, the information or indictment being
8 dismissed, the person being acquitted after a second or subsequent trial, or
9 through the granting of a pardon.
10 (b)(1) If the State challenges the exoneration of a person entitled to bring
11 an action under this subchapter, the limitations period shall not commence
12 until the challenge is finally resolved.
13 (2) If a person entitled to bring an action under this subchapter is not
14 provided the notice required by section 5577 of this title, the person shall have
15 an additional year within which to bring the action.
16 § 5577. NOTICE OF RIGHT OF ACTION
