4 Subject: Conservation and development; fish and wildlife conservation;

6 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to make
7 technical changes to the uniform point system statute for fish and wildlife
8 violations. This bill would grant the Commissioner of Fish and Wildlife
9 discretion to determine whether annual hunting licenses expire on December
10 31 of the year for which they were issued or 365 days after the date they were
11 issued. This bill also would grant the Department of Fish and Wildlife the
12 ability to assess a fee for additional tags or permits that are not otherwise
13 identified in statute.
14 An act relating to technical corrections to fish and wildlife statutes

2 Sec. 2. 10 V.S.A. § 4263 is amended to read:
3 § 4263. EXPIRATION; NONTRANSFERABILITY
4 Licenses shall not be transferable and shall be dated when issued. Annual
5 licenses shall be valid from January 1 of the calendar year for which they are
6 issued or from the day of purchase and issue in that year as certified by the
7 issuing agent. All annual licenses shall expire on December 31 of the year for
8 which they were issued or 365 days after the date on which they are issued, as
9 determined by the Commissioner. Term licenses shall be valid only for the
10 time period certified on the license form by the issuing agent. Any licenses
11 may be sold prior to the date that they are to become effective.
