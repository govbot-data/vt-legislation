1 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
2 Sec. 1. FINDINGS
3 The General Assembly finds that:
4 (1) Vermont has a compelling interest in protecting its residents,
5 especially students, educators, and public employees, from both antisemitism
6 and censorship.
7 (2) Antisemitism is a real and growing threat that must be clearly
8 defined to ensure accountability for bias-motivated acts, not conflated with
9 lawful expression.
10 (3) The First Amendment to the U.S. Constitution prohibits government
11 actors from favoring or disfavoring particular political viewpoints.
12 (4) In recent years, laws or policies in other states and institutions have
13 led to the suppression of lawful advocacy, particularly in support of Palestinian
14 human rights.
15 (5) Jewish, Muslim, Arab, and other communities in Vermont have
16 expressed concern about the use of vague or overly broad antisemitism
17 definitions that silence critical political discourse.
18 (6) Vermont reaffirms that educational institutions must be both safe
19 and intellectually open environments where civil rights and free speech
20 coexist.

1 Sec. 2. 16 V.S.A. § 570 is amended to read:
2 § 570. HARASSMENT, HAZING, AND BULLYING PREVENTION
3 POLICIES
4 (a) State policy. It is the policy of the State of Vermont that all Vermont
5 educational institutions provide safe, orderly, civil, and positive learning
6 environments. Harassment, hazing, and bullying have no place and will not be
7 tolerated in Vermont schools. No Vermont student should feel threatened or
8 be discriminated against while enrolled in a Vermont school.
9 (b) Prevention policies. Each school board shall develop, adopt, ensure the
10 enforcement of, and make available in the manner described under subdivision
11 563(1) of this title harassment, hazing, and bullying prevention policies that
12 shall be at least as stringent as model policies developed by the Secretary. Any
13 school board that fails to adopt one or more of these policies shall be presumed
14 to have adopted the most current model policy or policies published by the
15 Secretary.
16 (c) Notice. Annually, prior to the commencement of curricular and
17 cocurricular activities, the school board shall provide notice of the policy and
18 procedures developed under this subchapter to students, custodial parents or
19 guardians of students, and staff members, including reference to the
20 consequences of misbehavior contained in the plan required by section 1161a
21 of this title. Notice to students shall be in age-appropriate language and should
