10 § 4091. BABY FOOD PRODUCTS
11 (a) As used in this section:
12 (1) “Baby food product” means any food manufactured, packaged, and
13 labeled in a jar, pouch, tub, or box sold specifically for babies and children
14 younger than two years of age. “Baby food product” does not include infant
15 formula.
16 (2) “Final baby food product” means the finished baby food product and
17 not the constituent ingredients.
18 (3) “Infant formula” means a commercially available milk-based or soy-
19 based powder, concentrated liquid, or ready-to-feed substitute for human breast
20 milk that is intended for infant consumption.

1 (8) “Toxic heavy metal” means arsenic, cadmium, lead, and mercury.
2 (9) “URL” means a uniform resource locator.
3 (10) “U.S. FDA” means the U.S. Food and Drug Administration.
4 (b) A person shall not sell, distribute, or offer for sale any baby food
5 product in the State that contains a toxic heavy metal that exceeds the
6 regulatory limits established by the U.S. FDA. The provisions of this
7 subsection shall not restrict the continued sale of inventory in stock before
8 January 1, 2027.
9 (c) A manufacturer of a baby food sold or distributed in the State shall test
10 a representative sample of each production aggregate of the manufacturer’s
11 final baby food product for toxic heavy metals. Testing of a baby food product
12 shall be conducted by a proficient laboratory at least once a month. A
13 manufacturer of baby food may test the final baby food product before
14 packaging individual units for sale or distribution. Upon request of the Office
15 of the Attorney General, a manufacturer shall provide the results of the test
16 conducted pursuant to this subsection.
17 (d)(1) Without requiring the provision of a universal product code or proof
18 of purchase, for each baby food product sold, manufactured, delivered, held, or
19 offered for sale in the State, a manufacturer of baby food shall make publicly
20 available on its website for the duration of the product shelf life of a final baby
21 food product, plus one month:

1 Sec. 3. INFANT FORMULA; STOCK SUPPLY
2 The provisions of Sec. 2 (18 V.S.A. § 4091) of this act shall not restrict the
3 continued sale of infant formula inventory in stock in Vermont prior to the
4 effective date of Sec. 2 of this act pursuant to Sec. 4(b) of this act.
5 Sec. 4. EFFECTIVE DATES
6 (a) This section, Sec. 1 (18 V.S.A. chapter 82), and Sec. 3 (infant formula;
7 stock supply) shall take effect on January 1, 2027.
8 (b) Sec. 2 (18 V.S.A. § 4091) shall take effect upon the Attorney General’s
9 written confirmation to the Speaker of the House and to the President Pro
10 Tempore of the Senate, which shall be posted on the General Assembly’s
11 website, that a law has taken effect in California or two other states with
12 requirements substantially comparable to the requirements of this act regarding
13 all of the following:
14 (1) the prohibition on the sale and distribution of infant formula that
15 contains a toxic heavy metal exceeding U.S. Food and Drug Administration
16 limits;
17 (2) the required testing of infant formula sold or distributed in the state
18 for toxic heavy metals; and
19 (3) the labeling of infant formula and the provision of information about
20 toxic heavy metals in infant formula.
