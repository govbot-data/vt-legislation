1 S.327
2 An act relating to economic development
3 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
4 Sec. 1. PURPOSE
5 This act proposes to strengthen and support Vermont’s businesses and
6 promote long-term economic growth by investing in the economic
7 development and vitality of its downtowns and village centers, providing key
8 financial support, technical assistance, and incentives for Vermont businesses
9 of all sizes and at all stages of development. This act also requires the
10 Commissioner of Economic Development to research and create an inventory
11 of the resources available to businesses to assist with growth and development,
12 the Office of Workforce Strategy and Development to study the feasibility of
13 establishing a new culinary institute in the State, and the Vermont Association
14 of Planning and Development Agencies to study the short- and long-term
15 solutions to better connect the economies of New York and Vermont.

2 Sec. 10. CULINARY INSTITUTE; STUDY; REPORT
3 (a) Purpose. The State of Vermont lost a significant contributor to its
4 culinary workforce pipeline development when the New England Culinary
5 Institute closed during the COVID-19 pandemic. The General Assembly finds
6 that the establishment of a new culinary institute is critical for long-term
7 workforce needs in the food, tourism, and hospitality sectors, sectors that are
8 significant for the economic health of the State.
9 (b) Task. The Office of Workforce Strategy and Development shall engage
10 with the stakeholders set forth in subsection (c) of this section to determine
11 how best to establish a new culinary institute in Vermont by doing the
12 following:
13 (1) research suitable options for the location of a culinary institute;
14 (2) determine which college or organization should stand up and
15 administer the culinary institute;
16 (3) determine to what extent the General Assembly is needed to help
17 fund and establish the culinary institute;
18 (4) begin establishing relationships with restaurants in Vermont that
19 have or will have workforce needs;
20 (5) gauge the interest from private investors to determine whether there
21 is interest in private funding for a culinary institute; and
