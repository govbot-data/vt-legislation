Act No. 168 (H.944). An act relating to the fiscal year 2027 Transportation Program and
miscellaneous changes to laws related to transportation
Subjects: Transportation; speed limits; Agency of Transportation; bonding;
mileage-based user fee; Drive Electric Vermont; Public Transit Advisory Council;
annual Transportation Program; Transportation Board; Transportation
Alternatives Grant Program; Municipal Equipment and Vehicle Loan Fund; State
highway design standards; bridge inspections; public-private partnerships; Green
Mountain Transit Authority; Caledonia County State Airport; electric vehicle
supply equipment (EVSE); intelligent speed assistance; log drives
This act:
• Transportation Program. Adopts the Agency of Transportation’s (Agency or
AOT) Proposed Fiscal Year 2027 Transportation Program except as amended
in the act and defines terms used throughout the act.
• Fiscal Year 2026 Transportation Investments Intended to Reduce
Transportation-Related Greenhouse Gas Emissions, Reduce Fossil Fuel Use,
and Save Vermont Households Money. Summarizes certain transportation
investments.
• Paving. Allocates an additional $1,700,000.00 for Statewide District Leveling.
• State Highway Bridges. Replaces $1,750,000.00 of project funding from the
Transportation Fund with $1,750,000.00 from the Transportation Infrastructure
Bond Fund.
• Transportation Infrastructure Bonds. Requires the Agency to report to the
General Assembly in January 2027 regarding projects that are eligible to be
funded with transportation infrastructure bonds that are not in the Fiscal Year
2028 Transportation Program and could be advanced to construction in fiscal
year 2028 or 2029 if bonding was authorized for project funding.
• Mileage-Based User Fee. Establishes the mileage-based user fee program in
the Department of Motor Vehicles. Under the program, electric vehicles (EVs)
will pay a fee of 1.4 cents per mile traveled during an annual reporting period,
with a maximum annual fee of $178.00. The act provides that currently
registered EVs will begin their initial reporting period with the EV’s annual
inspection in 2027, while newly registered EVs will commence their initial
reporting period upon registration in Vermont. Owners and lessees who renew
their EV’s registration prior to the completion of the initial reporting period
will pay a one-time road usage charge at the time of registration that is equal to
$89.00 for a one-year registration and $178.00 for a two-year registration,
which will be applied as a credit against the amount of the mileage-based user

fee due for that EV. For EVs whose registration will not be renewed until after
the first reporting period, the most recent EV infrastructure fee paid for that
EV will be applied as a credit against the amount of the mileage-based user fee
due. In addition, the act requires the Agency and the Department of Motor
Vehicles to submit reports to the General Assembly regarding the
implementation of the program, a plan and proposed legislation to expand the
program to plug-in hybrid vehicles, additional methods for owners and lessees
to track vehicle miles traveled, and other potential updates to the program. The
act also makes amendments to the infrastructure fee for plug-in hybrid
vehicles, requires the Agency and the Department of Motor Vehicles to
conduct outreach and education and to solicit feedback regarding the mileage-
based user fee program, and requires the Department of Taxes to examine
potential options to replace the sales tax on the sale of electricity through
public charging stations with other mechanisms generating Transportation
Fund revenue from public charging stations.
• Repeal of Municipal Equipment and Vehicle Loan Fund Rules. Repeals rules
related to the former Municipal Heavy Equipment Loan Fund.
• Statement of Policy; Highways and Bridges. Makes technical amendments to
clarify provisions relating to Vermont standards for roadway design and the
application of certain federal signage requirements to roadways with lower
design speeds.
• Agency of Transportation; Duties. Increases the maximum contract amount
for which the Secretary of Transportation may waive bonding requirements
and permits the Secretary to waive contract bonding requirements for
temporary stabilization work during an emergency.
• Bridge Inspection; Posting; Closure. Brings State statutes into compliance
with the National Bridge Inspection Standards regarding inspection, posting,
and closure of bridges and creates a penalty of up to $1,000.00 for violating a
bridge posting or closure.
• Public Transit Advisory Council. Makes various changes to the membership
of the Public Transit Advisory Council.
• Green Mountain Transit Authority. Permits the Green Mountain Transit
Authority to include voluntary member contributions, grants, donations, and
other nonassessment revenue in its annual budget.
• Public-Private Partnership Sunset Extension. Extends AOT’s authority to
enter into public-private partnerships until July 1, 2029.
• Transportation Board. Limits the Transportation Board’s authority to hear
Section 1111 permit appeals to appeals by the applicant or permittee.
• Transportation Alternatives Grant Program. Permanently increases the
maximum grant amount to $600,000.00, with a temporary increase to
$1,200,000.00 for fiscal year 2027. This act also eliminates the requirement
that 50 percent of Grant Program funds be reserved for environmental

• Consultation Regarding Municipal Programs. Requires AOT to continue to
work with stakeholders on studies from 2025 Acts and Resolves No. 43
regarding the cancellation of locally managed projects and identifying potential
efficiencies related to town highway aid and municipal grant programs, as well
as to work with stakeholders to develop recommendations related to
simplifying and clarifying the statutes related to establishing municipal speed
limits.
• Drive Electric Vermont. Appropriates $242,000.00 to the Agency to continue
the Agency’s work with Drive Electric Vermont.
• Caledonia County State Airport. Amends requirements for the sale of
Caledonia County State Airport and extends authority for the State to sell or
lease the airport to November 1, 2027.
• Medical Transports. Authorizes AOT to provide grants to public transit
agencies to support recruitment and retention of volunteer drivers and mobility
management activities related to nonemergency medical transports.
• Real-Time Status of Public EVSE. Requires public electric vehicle charging
stations that are installed or reconditioned after September 30, 2026, to make
certain real-time information available to third-party software developers.
• EVSE Installation in Common Interest Communities. Prohibits common
interest communities from placing unreasonable restrictions on the installation
of EV chargers in certain locations.
• Intelligent Speed Assistance. Requires the Department of Motor Vehicles to
report to the General Assembly regarding the potential for implementing an
intelligent speed assistance program in Vermont.
• Repeals; Log Drives. Repeals outdated provisions related to log drives.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on April 30, 2026
