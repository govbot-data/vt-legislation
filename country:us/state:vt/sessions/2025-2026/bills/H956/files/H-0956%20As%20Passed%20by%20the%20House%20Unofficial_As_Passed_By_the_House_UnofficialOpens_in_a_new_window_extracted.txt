2 An act relating to approval of an amendment to the charter of the City of
3 Burlington relating to the Office of Racial Equity, Inclusion, and Belonging
4 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
5 Sec. 1. CHARTER AMENDMENT APPROVAL
6 The General Assembly approves the amendment to the charter of the City
7 of Burlington as set forth in this act. The voters of the City approved the
8 proposal of amendment on March 3, 2026.
9 Sec. 2. 24 App. V.S.A. chapter 3 is amended to read:
10 CHAPTER 3. CITY OF BURLINGTON
11 * * *
12 Subchapter 8. General Provisions
13 * * *
14 § 374. OFFICE OF RACIAL EQUITY, INCLUSION, AND BELONGING
15 (a) The City of Burlington shall form the Office of Racial Equity,
16 Inclusion, and Belonging (REIB) and establish a Director of Racial Equity,
17 Inclusion, and Belonging.
18 (b) The Director of REIB role shall be a permanent position in the City,
19 selected and hired in accordance with City of Burlington Human Resources
20 policies and procedures, and shall report directly to the Mayor.

1 (c) The REIB shall be under the supervision, direction, and control of the
2 Director, subject to the orders and ordinances of the City Council.
3 (d) The Director of REIB shall have all of the powers, duties, and
4 responsibilities as are provided in the Director position description.
5 (e) Subject to the orders and ordinances of the City Council, the Mayor and
6 the Director of REIB shall develop and implement a comprehensive racial
7 equity, inclusion, and belonging strategy for the City of Burlington.
8 (f) In furtherance of this work, the Director of REIB shall, subject to the
9 orders and ordinances of the City Council, have and implement the following
10 rights, powers, and duties in collaboration with all City departments:
11 (1) to work on behalf of the City to advance equity and social justice by
12 championing diversity, inclusion, belonging, and antiracism;
13 (2) to develop, coordinate, implement, and administer racial equity and
14 inclusion strategies and projects for the City;
15 (3) to provide consultation for and recommend to City departments, the
16 Mayor’s office, and the City Council policy and program changes to better
17 advance racial equity and inclusion in the City;
18 (4) to enhance the representation in City government for individuals
19 who hold underserved community identities, through decision-making bodies,
20 community engagement initiatives, and policy development processes; and
