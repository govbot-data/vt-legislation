State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 156
Offered by Representative Noyes of Wolcott
House concurrent resolution honoring Timothy McNamara for his outstanding public service in
environmental and municipal law enforcement
Whereas, for over three decades of law-enforcement-related employment, Tim McNamara’s peers
recognized the consistently high caliber of their colleague’s professionalism, and
Whereas, Tim McNamara began his tenure at the Agency of Natural Resources (ANR) in 1992 as a
temporary underground storage tank inspector in the Department of Environmental Conservation, and in 1995,
he accepted a permanent position, resulting in his promotion to assistant hazardous materials specialist, and
Whereas, frequently, the violations Tim McNamara investigated were referred to an ANR environmental
enforcement officer (EEO), and he developed a strong interest in a law-enforcement-focused career, and
Whereas, in 1997, Tim McNamara pursued his passion and joined the EEO’s ranks, completing the requisite
training for the performance of his new assignments, and
Whereas, soon after becoming an EEO, Tim McNamara played a major role in an enforcement proceeding
following a fatal hazardous waste explosion that culminated in felony convictions, and
Whereas, in the years that followed, Tim McNamara brought his expertise and skill to investigations
centered on the illegal disposal of solid and hazardous waste for profit, the illegal burning of materials, the
unpermitted discharges of pollutants into State waters, and the submission of falsified records to State
authorities, and
Whereas, his proficiency as an EEO attracted the notice of municipal law enforcement officials, and in 2006,
Tim McNamara embarked, initially with trepidation but ultimately with great success, on a parallel part-time
second career serving as a local police officer in the towns of Weathersfield (2005–2006 and 2012–2022) and
Springfield (2006–2018), and
Whereas, in 2015, the Vermont Criminal Justice Training Council (VCJTC) issued him a Level 2C law
enforcement certification, and he also earned VCJTC’s authorization to investigate State criminal
environmental statutes, and
Whereas, after 33 years, in 2025, Tim McNamara concluded his exemplary ANR investigatory career,
now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly honors Timothy McNamara for his outstanding public service in environmental
and municipal law enforcement, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Tim McNamara.
