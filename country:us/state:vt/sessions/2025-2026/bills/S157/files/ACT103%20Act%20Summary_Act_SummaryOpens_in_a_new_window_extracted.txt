Act No. 103 (S.157). An act relating to recovery residence certification
Subjects: Certification; health; substance use disorder; recovery residence
This act requires that the Department of Health’s Division of Substance Use Programs
assume responsibility for recovery residence services in the State. Specifically, the act
directs the Department to file an initial proposed rule with the Secretary of State by
September 1, 2027, for the purpose of establishing a voluntary recovery residence
certification program and requires that the permanent rule be adopted by December 1,
2028.
This act amends an existing law that enables a recovery residence to immediately exit
or transfer a resident if certain conditions are met. It removes the preexisting July 1,
2026, sunset for this section of law. It also adds and amends conditions for the immediate
exit or transfer of a resident. The amended condition relates to resident behavior that
impacts the health or safety of other individuals residing, working, or volunteering at the
recovery residence. The new conditions are related to the provision of written or
electronic notice to a resident containing the date and rationale for a temporary removal
or transfer and options for returning to the recovery residence and the establishment of a
grievance process. This act also allows a recovery residence employee to enter the
recovery residence at reasonable times as necessary to carry out functions related to the
operation of the recovery residence.
Effective Date: July 1, 2026
