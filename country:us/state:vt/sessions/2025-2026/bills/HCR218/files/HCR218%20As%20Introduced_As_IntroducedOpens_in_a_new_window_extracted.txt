State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 218
Offered by All Members of the House
House concurrent resolution recognizing April 2026 as the Month of the Military Child in Vermont
Whereas, the children of members of the U.S. military, who number approximately 1.6 million,
exemplify strength, adaptability, and a global outlook, reflective of their unique experiences in military
families, and they face long deployments and frequent separations from loved ones with courage and
grace, and
Whereas, in 1986, the U.S. Department of Defense first designated April as the Month of the Military
Child, and this annual event is part of a year-round initiative aimed at enhancing the lives of these
youths, and
Whereas, Purple Up! For Military Kids Day is an important aspect of the Month of the Military
Child, and on this special day, Americans are encouraged to wear purple, a color that symbolizes all
branches of the military and the fortitude, resilience, and sacrifices of militarily connected youth, and
Whereas, although April 10 and April 15 are both widely observed as the dates for this colorful
celebration, since the General Assembly is commemorating the Month of the Military Child today, April
21, 2026, it is an ideal day for wearing purple in support of military children, and
Whereas, the month of April is designated for the recognition of military children, who, in reality,
serve alongside their families, and these youngsters’ personal adaptability to changing circumstances,
which enables their parents to protect and defend our nation, is worthy of applause and recognition, now
therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly recognizes April 2026 as the Month of the Military Child in Vermont,
and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to the Vermont
National Guard Office of Child & Youth Services.
