6 Subject: Crimes; homicide; abuse of a corpse
7 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to create a new
8 felony statute for an individual who abuses a corpse. Specifically, this bill
9 makes it a felony for an individual to transport or destroy a corpse without
10 legal authorization. Additionally, this bill creates an aggravated felony penalty
11 for actions taken against a corpse to conceal a crime or otherwise avoid the
12 consequences of the commission of a crime.
13 An act relating to creating a felony penalty for abuse of a corpse
14 An act relating to abuse of the dead body of a person
It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
15 Sec. 1. 13 V.S.A. § 2312 is added to read:
16 § 2312. ABUSE OF A CORPSE
17 (a) A person is guilty of abuse of a corpse if the person, without legal
18 authorization, knowingly:
19 (1) transports, buries, or otherwise hides a corpse; or

1 (2) burns, mutilates, disfigures, dismembers, or otherwise destroys a

3 (b) A person is guilty of aggravated abuse of a corpse if the person
4 intentionally:
5 (1) transports, buries, or otherwise hides a corpse to conceal a crime or
6 avoid apprehension, prosecution, or conviction of a crime;
7 (2) burns, mutilates, disfigures, dismembers, or otherwise destroys a
8 corpse to conceal a crime or avoid apprehension, prosecution, or conviction of
9 a crime; or
10 (3) perpetrates or attempts to perpetrate sexual assault against a corpse.
11 (c)(1) A person who commits the crime of abuse of a corpse shall be
12 imprisoned for not more than five years or fined not more than $5,000.00, or
13 both.
14 (2) A person who commits the crime of aggravated abuse of a corpse
15 shall be imprisoned for not less than five years and a maximum term of 10
16 years and may be fined not more than $10,000.00.
17 Sec. 2. EFFECTIVE DATE

(a) No person shall, knowingly without legal authorization, intentionally
burn, mutilate, disfigure, dismember, or destroy the dead body of a person.
(b)(1) No person shall intentionally bury, transport, hide, conceal, burn,
mutilate, disfigure, dismember, or destroy the dead body of a person for the
purpose of concealing a crime or avoiding apprehension, prosecution, or
conviction of a crime.
(2) No person shall commit sexual conduct upon the dead body of a
person.
(c)(1) A person who violates subsection (a) of this section shall be
imprisoned not more than five years or fined not more than $5,000.00, or both.
(2) A person who violates subsection (b) of this section shall be
imprisoned not more than 15 years or fined not more than $10,000.00, or both.
(d) As used in this section:
(1) “Dead body of a person” does not include the cremated remains of a
person.
(2) “Sexual conduct” means any of the following committed against the
dead body of a person:
(A) any conduct involving contact between the penis and the vulva,
the penis and the penis, the penis and the anus, the mouth and the penis, the
mouth and the anus, the vulva and the vulva, or the mouth and the vulva;

Sec. 2. 18 V.S.A. § 5211 is amended to read:
§ 5211. UNAUTHORIZED BURIAL OR REMOVAL; PENALTY
A person who buries, entombs, transports, or removes the dead body of a
person without a burial-transit permit so to do, or in any other manner or at
any other time or place than as specified in such permit, shall be imprisoned
not more than five years or fined not more than $1,000.00, or both.
Sec. 3. EFFECTIVE DATE
