State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 180
Offered by Representatives Black of Essex, Dodge of Essex, Dolan of Essex Junction, Garofano of
Essex, Houghton of EssexJunction, and Steady of Milton
Offered by Senators Baruth, Gulick, Mattos, and Vyhovsky
House concurrent resolution congratulating the 2025 Essex High School Hornets on winning the
State boys’ volleyball championship
Whereas, in recent years, the Essex Hornets boys’ volleyball team’s skill has been reconfirmed
repeatedly with the school’s five-time representation in the State championship game, but, regrettably,
victory always eluded them, and
Whereas, in 2025, the team’s fortunes changed as first-seeded and undefeated Essex again earned a
championship berth and faced the second-ranked Mount Mansfield Union High School Cougars in a
contest at St. Michael’s College in Colchester, and
Whereas, in a tightly played first set, Essex emerged on top 25–22, although the Cougars rebounded
in the second set 25–18, guaranteeing at least a fourth set of play, and
Whereas, in the third set, the reenergized Hornets proceeded to defeat the Cougars convincingly 25–
15, and despite a final Cougar surge, Essex prevailed 25–22 in the fourth set, clinching a 3–1 game
victory and the long-awaited first State crown, and
Whereas, the jubilant Hornets (16–0), who emerged triumphant, were Coen Giles, Thomas Trombley,
Maxim Katsnelson, Aaditya Pradhan, Riley Anger, Malx Eckman, Cooper Yandow, Rohan Rai, Gregory
Maiberger, Ishack Magar, Patrick Jordan, Evan Hughes, and Lorenzo Ayoub, and
Whereas, cohead coaches Dylan Lamere and Gary Randall proved great strategists for the team, now
therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly congratulates the 2025 Essex High School Hornets on winning the State
boys’ volleyball championship, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Essex High
School.
