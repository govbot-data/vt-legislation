State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 240
Offered by Representatives Bartholomew of Hartland and Burrows of West Windsor
House concurrent resolution congratulating Stephen R. Bissette on his designation as the sixth Vermont
Cartoonist Laureate
Whereas, in a nationally unique collaboration between the Center for Cartoon Studies in White River
Junction and the Vermont Arts Council, the Vermont Cartoonist Laureate program advances the recognition of
comics as a distinctive and powerful artistic medium, spanning graphic novels, journalism, children’s literature,
and other forms of storytelling, and
Whereas, the graphic artists previously so honored were James Kochalka, Ed Koren, Alison Bechdel, Rick
Veitch, and, most recently, Tillie Walden, and
Whereas, the newly designated sixth Vermont Cartoonist Laureate is Stephen R. Bissette of Windsor, a
Waterbury native and a 1973 graduate of Harwood Union High School (and a 2025 inductee into the school’s
hall of fame), and, professionally, he is a proud alumnus of the Joe Kubert School of Cartoon and Graphic Art’s
first graduating class, an internationally recognized and honored cartoonist and publisher, a dedicated artists’
advocate who was a signatory of the 1988 Creator’s Bill of Rights, and a founding faculty member of the
Center for Cartoon Studies, and
Whereas, in 1976, prior to enrolling at the Kubert School, Stephen R. Bissette began his comics publishing
career with Abyss, and his reputation as a leading comic book artist and groundbreaking contributor to the
medium’s horror genre were confirmed when he served as the cocreator and an artist (with Alan Moore and
John Totleben) of Saga of the Swamp Thing, a comic series that introduced the enduring character of John
Constantine, and
Whereas, his career contributions to comic book creation and publication have ranged from the dinosaur epic
Tyrant, the adaptation of Steven Spielberg’s film 1941 as a graphic novel, Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtle
projects, and as the cocreator and coeditor (again, in collaboration with John Totleben) of the influential Taboo
anthology, and he introduced an important outlet for independent comic book publishing and a home for
boundary-pushing creators, and
Whereas, Stephen R. Bissette, whose graphic artistry has extended to many unique applications, is the
winner of top professional honors, including multiple Jack Kirby Comics Industry Awards, the Inkpot Award,
and the Eisner Award, all outstanding credentials for this career recognition, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly congratulates Stephen R. Bissette on his designation as the sixth Vermont
Cartoonist Laureate, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Stephen R. Bissette,
the Center for Cartoon Studies, and the Vermont Arts Council.
