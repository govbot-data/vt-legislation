2 An act relating to health insurance coverage for biomarker testing
3 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
4 Sec. 1. HEALTH INSURANCE AND MEDICAID COVERAGE FOR
5 BIOMARKER TESTING; DEPARTMENT OF FINANCIAL
6 REGULATION; AGENCY OF HUMAN SERVICES; REPORTS
7 (a) As used in this section:
8 (1) “Biomarker” means a characteristic that is objectively measured and
9 evaluated as an indicator of normal biological processes, pathogenic processes,
10 or pharmacologic responses to a specific therapeutic intervention, including
11 known gene-drug interactions for medications being considered for use or
12 already being administered. Biomarkers include gene mutations,
13 characteristics of genes, and protein expression.
14 (2) “Biomarker testing” means the analysis of a patient’s tissue, blood,
15 or other biospecimen for the presence of a biomarker. Biomarker testing
16 includes single-analyte tests; multiplex panel tests; protein expression analysis;
17 and whole exome, whole genome, and whole transcriptome sequencing.
18 (3) “Consensus statements” means statements developed by an
19 independent, multidisciplinary panel of experts utilizing a transparent
20 methodology and reporting structure and with a conflict of interest policy.
21 These statements are aimed at specific clinical circumstances and the
