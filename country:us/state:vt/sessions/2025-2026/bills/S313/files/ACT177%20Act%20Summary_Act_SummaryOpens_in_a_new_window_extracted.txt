Act No. 177 (S.313). An act relating to transforming Vermont’s career technical education
system
Subjects: Education; career technical education
This act makes several updates to the laws that govern Vermont’s career technical
education (CTE) system. First, this act states the General Assembly’s intent to transform
Vermont’s CTE system by providing universal access to CTE; enabling flexible delivery
models to offer multiple pathways for students to access CTE; aligning the CTE system
with workforce needs; creating a sustainable and student-centered funding system that
removes disincentives for participation; exploring the viability and impact of CTE centers
becoming diploma-conferring institutions or comprehensive high schools; maintaining a
strong adult CTE system; and coordinating CTE governance with the ongoing education
transformation process. It requires the Agency of Education, in collaboration with the
Vermont Association of Career and Technical Directors and the Vermont
Superintendents Association, to issue guidance, on or before September 18, 2026, on
several topics to provide clarity to the field prior to updates to the CTE rules. This act
requires the State Board of Education to review CTE rules and procedures not less than
every five years and to update the rules and procedures as necessary. It requires the
Agency of Education to submit a written report to the House Committees on Commerce
and Economic Development and on Education and the Senate Committees on Economic
Development, Housing and General Affairs and on Education with recommendations for
CTE educator endorsement requirements that will balance the need for increased access
to CTE educators with industry expertise with the need to ensure CTE educators are
qualified to provide education in their specialty fields without unintentionally creating
additional barriers or complexity; a pre-enforcement intervention pathway for disputes or
disagreements in the field regarding interpretation and implementation of CTE laws;
updates to the Flexible Pathways Initiative that alleviate statewide inconsistencies; and
ensuring Adult Diploma participants have access to the education programs that best
serve their needs, along with the recommendations for how to fund such access. This act
states the General Assembly’s intent to move toward a model of comprehensive high
schools that would offer all opportunities available to students within the public
education system within school building or one school campus, with a single budget for
both CTE education and general education. This act requires a school board to apply
credits or proficiencies earned for CTE programs toward graduation requirements.
This act also makes several updates to Vermont’s labor law. It adds a citation to the
Code of Federal Regulations regarding the employer’s burden to demonstrate that an
employee did not work the required number of hours to be eligible to take Parental and
Family Leave Act under Vermont law. This act lists the sources of supporting
documentation that survivors of domestic violence, sexual assault, and stalking may rely
upon to meet the definition of crime victim, a protected category under the Fair

Employment Practices Act. This act repeals a provision in the Fair Employment
Practices Act allowing institutions of higher education to require tenured faculty to retire
at 70 years of age. This act removes elected and municipal officers from the definition of
employee for purposes of Vermont’s minimum wage and overtime laws. Finally, this act
renders certain provisions in contracts or agreements with health care providers void and
unenforceable.
