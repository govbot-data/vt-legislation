Act No. 137 (H.921). An act relating to alcoholic beverages
Subjects: Licensing; fourth-class licenses; special venue serving permits; alcoholic
beverages, cannabis, and tobacco; manufacturers and rectifiers; Board of Liquor
and Lottery; special events permits
This act amends various sections of Title 7 to increase the term length for members of
the Board of Liquor and Lottery, increase the total volume of alcoholic beverages that
may be served to a retail customer at a tasting room and retail shop under a fourth-class
license, increase the number of fourth-class licensed locations at which a manufacturer
may sell beverages produced by other manufacturers, eliminate the specific hours of sale
for sales authorized pursuant to 7 V.S.A. § 230 and revert to the hours of sale applicable
to licensees pursuant to 7 V.S.A. § 62, conform the special event permits statute to
contain the same local control requirements as the definition of “special event permit”
under 7 V.S.A. § 2, decrease the notice period for retail alcoholic beverage tasting
permits from five days to one business day, authorize licensed manufacturers of malt
beverages to self-distribute not more than 3,000 barrels of malt beverages annually to
first- and second-class licensees, remove the requirement that an applicant or holder of a
solicitor’s license pay licensing fees by certified check, eliminate the prospective repeal
of the 2024 special venue serving permit amendments, and authorize the holder of a
caterer’s license to host not more than five functions per calendar year located on the
license holder’s own first-, first- and third-, or second-class licensed premises.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on June 15, 2026
