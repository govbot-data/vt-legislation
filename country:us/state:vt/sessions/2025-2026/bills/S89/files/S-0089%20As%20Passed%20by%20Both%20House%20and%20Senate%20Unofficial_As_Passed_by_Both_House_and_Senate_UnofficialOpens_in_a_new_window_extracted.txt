5 Sec. 2. 20 V.S.A. § 3172 is amended to read:
6 § 3172. EMERGENCY PERSONNEL SURVIVORS BENEFIT REVIEW
7 BOARD
8 (a)(1) There is created the Emergency Personnel Survivors Benefit Review
9 Board, which shall consist of the State Treasurer or designee, the Attorney
10 General or designee, the Chief Fire Service Training Officer of the Vermont
11 Fire Service Training Council or designee, and one member of the public to
12 represent the interests of emergency personnel appointed by the Governor for a
13 term of two years.
14 (2) Survivors of emergency personnel, employed by or who volunteer
15 for the State of Vermont, a county or municipality of the State, or a nonprofit
16 entity that provides services in the State, who die in the line of duty or of an
17 occupation-related illness may request the Board award a monetary benefit
18 under section 3173 of this title chapter, except survivors of emergency
19 personnel as defined in subdivisions 3171(3)(C)–(F) of this chapter may
20 request the monetary benefit only for deaths that occur on or after July 1, 2026.

1 (3) The Board shall be responsible for determining whether to award
2 monetary benefits under section 3173 of this chapter. To assist the Board with
3 applications involving deaths from occupation-related illness, the Board may
4 pay reasonable fees from the Emergency Personnel Survivors Benefit Special
5 Fund for a medical expert and other services as necessary to review

7 (4) A decision to award monetary benefits shall be made by unanimous
8 vote of the Board and shall be made within 60 days after the receipt of all
9 information necessary to enable the Board to determine eligibility.
10 (5) The Board may request any information necessary for the exercise of
11 its duties under this section. Nothing in this section shall prevent the Board
12 from initiating the investigation or determination of a claim before being
