7 Subject: Education; courses of study; electronic devices
8 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to give students
9 or the parents or guardians of students in public schools and approved
10 independent schools the right to opt out of using electronic devices as part of
11 their education. It would also prohibit dismissing, suspending, disciplining,
12 reassigning, or transferring a teacher for choosing to teach a class or portions
13 of a class without the use of electronic devices.
14 An act relating to the right of a student or parent or guardian to opt out of
15 electronic device usage
16 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:

6 Sec. 2. 16 V.S.A. § 914 is added to read:
7 § 914. STUDENT AND PARENT OR GUARDIAN RIGHT OF REFUSAL;
8 ELECTRONIC DEVICE USAGE
9 (a) A student or parent or guardian of a student in a public elementary or
10 secondary school or an approved independent school shall have the right to
11 refuse to use an electronic device in order to perform work forming part of a
12 course of instruction.
13 (b) Each school district and approved independent school shall adopt and
14 implement policies regarding a student’s or parent’s or guardian’s right of
15 refusal under this section, which shall include:
16 (1) the process by which a student or parent or guardian may exercise
17 this right;
18 (2) alternative education methods through which a student exercising the
19 right under this section can learn and be assessed on material required by the
20 course; and
