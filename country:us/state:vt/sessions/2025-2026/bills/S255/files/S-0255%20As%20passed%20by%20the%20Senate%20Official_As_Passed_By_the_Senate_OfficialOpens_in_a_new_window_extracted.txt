11 An act relating to establishing a pilot Law Enforcement Governance
12 Council in Windham County
13 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
14 Sec. 1. DEFINITIONS
15 As used in this chapter:
16 (1) “Council” means the Windham County Law Enforcement
17 Governance Council established pursuant to this chapter.
18 (2) “Member municipality” means a municipality within Windham
19 County that has voted to participate in the Council and receive public safety
20 services pursuant to this chapter.

5 (5) “Sheriff” means the Sheriff of Windham County or designee.
6 Sec. 2. AUTHORIZATION AND ESTABLISHMENT
7 (a) Municipalities within Windham County are authorized to establish a
8 law enforcement governance council for the purpose of providing regional law
9 enforcement and related services during the pilot period.
10 (b) The Council shall be established when five or more eligible
11 municipalities vote to become member municipalities.
12 (c) The Council shall be established and associated operations and taxation
13 shall commence on either July 1, 2026, or the date on which five or more
14 member municipalities have voted to become member municipalities,
15 whichever occurs later.
16 Sec. 3. MEMBERSHIP
17 (a) A municipality within Windham County may join the Council by
18 majority vote at any annual or special town meeting warned for that purpose.

20 “Shall the Town of ________ join the Windham County Law Enforcement
21 Governance Council to receive regional law enforcement services, to be

12 (a) The Council shall consist of one representative from each member
13 municipality.
14 (b) The Council shall annually elect from its membership a chair, vice
15 chair, and secretary-treasurer.

3 Sec. 9. SUNSET; TERMINATION
4 (a) The Windham County Law Enforcement Governance Council shall
5 cease to exist on June 30, 2034.
6 (b) In the event of the number of member municipalities being reduced to
7 one, the Council shall be deemed terminated.
8 (c) On June 30, 2034, or at the time of the Council’s termination,
9 whichever occurs first:
10 (1) the Council shall cease operations;
11 (2) any remaining funds shall be returned proportionally by population
12 to current and prior member municipalities that were member municipalities
13 during the current fiscal year; and
14 (3) service agreements shall be terminated according to their terms.
15 Sec. 10. CONFORMING AMENDMENTS
16 Notwithstanding any provision of 24 V.S.A. chapter 5 to the contrary, the
17 Windham County Assistant Judges are authorized to include in the county
18 budget amounts approved by the Council pursuant to this act and to levy
19 differential taxrates on member and nonmember municipalities as necessary to
20 implement this act.
