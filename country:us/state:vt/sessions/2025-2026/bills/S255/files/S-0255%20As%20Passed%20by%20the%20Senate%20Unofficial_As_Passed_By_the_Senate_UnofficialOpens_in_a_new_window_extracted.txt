1 S.255
2 An act relating to establishing a pilot Law Enforcement Governance
3 Council in Windham County
4 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
5 Sec. 1. DEFINITIONS
6 As used in this chapter:
7 (1) “Council” means the Windham County Law Enforcement
8 Governance Council established pursuant to this chapter.
9 (2) “Member municipality” means a municipality within Windham
10 County that has voted to participate in the Council and receive public safety
11 services pursuant to this chapter.
12 (3) “Nonmember municipality” means a municipality within Windham
13 County that has not voted or voted not to participate in the Council.
14 (4) “Public safety services” means law enforcement, dispatch services,
15 animal control, and other related services as determined by the Council.
16 (5) “Sheriff” means the Sheriff of Windham County or designee.
17 Sec. 2. AUTHORIZATION AND ESTABLISHMENT
18 (a) Municipalities within Windham County are authorized to establish a
19 law enforcement governance council for the purpose of providing regional law
20 enforcement and related services during the pilot period.

1 (b) The Council shall be established when five or more eligible
2 municipalities vote to become member municipalities.
3 (c) The Council shall be established and associated operations and taxation
4 shall commence on either July 1, 2026, or the date on which five or more
5 member municipalities have voted to become member municipalities,
6 whichever occurs later.
7 Sec. 3. MEMBERSHIP
8 (a) A municipality within Windham County may join the Council by
9 majority vote of the legislative body of the municipality.
10 (b) Each member municipality shall:
11 (1) Have one representative, with one vote, on the Council. A member
12 municipality’s representative shall be a serving selectboard member of that
13 municipality, to be selected by the municipality’s selectboard.
14 (2) Receive law enforcement and related services as determined by the
15 Council.
16 (3) Pay its proportionate share of costs through the county tax
17 mechanism.
18 (c) A member municipality may withdraw from the Council by majority
19 vote of the legislative body of the municipality, with such withdrawal
20 becoming effective at the end of the next fiscal year following the vote.

1 Sec. 4. GOVERNANCE
2 (a) The Council shall consist of one representative from each member
3 municipality.
4 (b) The Council shall annually elect from its membership a chair, vice
5 chair, and secretary-treasurer.
6 (c) The Council shall meet at least quarterly and at such other times as
7 necessary. A majority of member municipalities shall constitute a quorum.
8 All meetings shall be warned and conducted in accordance with the Vermont
9 Open Meeting Law.
10 (d) The Council shall have the powers and duties to:
11 (1) adopt bylaws for the operation of the Council;
12 (2) determine the annual budget for law enforcement and related
13 services to be provided to member municipalities;
14 (3) establish the level and scope of services to be delivered to member
15 municipalities;
16 (4) develop standards and performance metrics for delivered law
17 enforcement and related services;
18 (5) monitor service delivery and evaluate program effectiveness;
19 (6) submit the approved budget to the Windham County Assistant
20 Judges for inclusion in the county budget; and
21 (7) enter into agreements necessary for the provision of services.

1 Sec. 5. BUDGET AND FINANCING
2 (a) Annually, the Council shall prepare and approve a budget for law
3 enforcement and related services for the following fiscal year. The budget
4 shall specify the total amount needed and the services to be provided. The
5 approved budget shall be submitted to the Windham County Assistant Judges
6 on or before December 1 of each year.
7 (b) The Assistant Judges shall include the Council’s approved budget as a
8 separate item in the county budget. The county treasurer shall levy and collect
9 a special assessment in proportion to each municipality’s population.
10 Nonmember municipalities shall not be assessed or charged for Council
11 services. Funds collected shall be segregated and used solely for the purposes
12 approved by the Council.
13 (c) The cost to each member municipality shall be determined by applying
14 the county tax rate necessary to raise the Council’s budget to each member’s
15 population.
16 (d) The county treasurer shall maintain separate accounts for Council
17 funds. The Council shall cause an annual audit to be performed by an
18 independent certified public accountant. Financial reports shall be provided
19 quarterly to member municipalities and the Assistant Judges.

1 Sec. 6. SERVICE DELIVERY
2 (a) The Windham County Sheriff’s Department shall provide law
3 enforcement and related services to member municipalities as specified by the
4 Council.
5 (b) Pursuant to 24 V.S.A. § 291a, the Council and Sheriff shall enter into
6 an annual service agreement specifying:
7 (1) the types and levels of service to be provided;
8 (2) standards and performance metrics for delivered law enforcement
9 services;
10 (3) reporting requirements; and
11 (4) allocation of resources among member municipalities.
12 (c) Law enforcement services provided shall be coordinated with State law
13 enforcement agencies, existing member and nonmember municipal law
14 enforcement agencies, and emergency services to ensure efficient resource
15 utilization.
16 Sec. 7. REPORTING AND EVALUATION
17 (a) Beginning in 2027, on or before January 31 of each year, the Council
18 shall submit a report to the House Committee on Government Operations and
19 Military Affairs, the Senate Committee on Government Operations, the
20 Windham County legislative delegation, and member municipalities
21 containing:

11 cease to exist on June 30, 2034.

13 one, the Council shall be deemed terminated.

15 whichever occurs first:
16 (1) the Council shall cease operations;
17 (2) any remaining funds shall be returned proportionally by population

20 (3) service agreements shall be terminated according to their terms.
