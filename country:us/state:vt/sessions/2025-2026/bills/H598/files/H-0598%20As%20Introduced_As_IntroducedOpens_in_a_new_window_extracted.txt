10 Sec. 2. 30 V.S.A. § 256 is added to read:
11 § 256. PORTABLE SOLAR ENERGY GENERATION DEVICES
12 (a) The installation of a portable solar energy generation device shall not be
13 required to comply with the requirements of section 248 of this chapter or be
14 required to obtain an interconnection agreement with an electric distribution
15 company.
16 (b) On or before September 10, 2026, the Public Utility Commission, in
17 consultation with the Division of Fire Safety and the electric distribution
18 companies, shall develop a simple notification form for customers who install
19 portable solar energy devices, which shall, at a minimum, include the name of
20 the customer and contact information, the address of installation, the
21 customer’s electric utility, and the meter number. The form shall be available

2 may require customers to submit the notification form to the company after

4 (c) An electric distribution company shall not require a customer using a
5 portable solar energy generation device to:
6 (1) obtain the company’s approval before installing or using the device;
7 (2) pay any fee or charge related to the device; or
8 (3) install any additional controls or equipment beyond what is
9 integrated into the device.
10 (d) A portable solar energy generation device shall not be eligible for net
11 metering.
12 (e) A portable solar energy device in a public building, as defined in 20
13 V.S.A. § 2730, shall be used in a manner that complies with all applicable
14 requirements of the most recent Fire and Building Safety Code adopted by the
