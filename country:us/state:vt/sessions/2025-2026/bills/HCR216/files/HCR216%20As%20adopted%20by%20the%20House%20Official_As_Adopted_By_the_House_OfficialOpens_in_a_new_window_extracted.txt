State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 216
Offered by Representative Coffin of Cavendish
Offered by Senators Clarkson, Major, and White
House concurrent resolution honoring Margo Caulfield for her exemplary and enormously appreciated
volunteer leadership in the Town of Cavendish
Whereas, for some Vermonters, admired civic service means appointment or election to a public office, and,
for others, this status is earned through a history of volunteerism, and
Whereas, a shining example of the latter category is Margo Caulfield of Cavendish, whose community
involvement encompasses the town’s past, present, and future, and
Whereas, from a historical perspective, Margo Caulfield manages the Cavendish Historical Society Museum,
curating the collection, initiating history projects for students, and, relatedly, leading grave-cleaning excursions
to familiarize youngsters with the town’s history, and
Whereas, in a contemporary context, she helps coordinate Cavendish’s Green Up Day program, recently
assisted in starting a successful summer farmers’ market, organized a popular townwide tag sale, and, with
friends, reinvigorated and enlivened the Vermont Golden Honey Festival, and
Whereas, an unusual project Margo Caulfield spearheaded is Culvert Crawlers, an undertaking conducted
with older students to improve the town’s water flow efficiency, and
Whereas, following both the devastating flooding from Tropical Storm Irene in 2011 and the July 2023
deluge, Margo Caulfield was an essential leader in the effort to ensure that food and shelter were available as
needed; thinking ahead, she worked with a local church to equip a shelter in anticipation of future
environmental emergencies; and as a further precaution, she distributes “go bags” in Cavendish, containing
essential items for use in future disaster situations, and
Whereas, Margo Caulfield is a passionate advocate for Cavendish’s seniors and others in need of community
support and for the extension of internet service to sections of town lacking digital coverage, and
Whereas, the residents of Cavendish are kept abreast of municipal and State news and emergency
information of local importance via Margo Caulfield’s weekly Cavendish Connects, which she distributes via
email, Facebook, and a website, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly honors Margo Caulfield for her exemplary and enormously appreciated
volunteer leadership in the Town of Cavendish, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Margo Caulfield.
