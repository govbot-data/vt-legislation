2 An act relating to prohibiting civil arrest in sensitive locations
3 The House proposes to the Senate to amend the bill by striking out all after
4 the enacting clause and inserting in lieu thereof the following:
5 Sec. 1. 12 V.S.A. § 3577 is amended to read:
6 § 3577. PRIVILEGE FROM ARREST
7 (a) The Governor, Lieutenant Governor, State Treasurer, Secretary of State,
8 Auditor of Accounts, Attorney General, and members of the General Assembly
9 and officers and witnesses whose duty it is to attend thereon, in all cases except
10 treason, felony, and breach of the peace, shall be privileged from arrest and
11 imprisonment during their necessary attendance on and in going to and
12 returning from the General Assembly.
13 (b) A party or witness in a cause pending in any court in the State or before
14 special masters, auditors, referees, or commissioners, and a witness in a
15 criminal cause pending in any such court, shall not be arrested, imprisoned, or
16 detained by virtue of civil process. Any witness summoned from outside the
17 State in a criminal cause, pending in any court within the State, shall be
18 privileged from the service of papers of any kind whatsoever, and from arrest
19 for any cause while going to, attending at, or returning from such court or trial
20 of such cause.
