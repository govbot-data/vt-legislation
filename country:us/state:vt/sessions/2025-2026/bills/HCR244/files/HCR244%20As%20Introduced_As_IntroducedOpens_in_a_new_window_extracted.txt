HouseconcurrentresolutionrecognizingtheweekofApril11–17, 2026, as BlackMaternalHealthWeekinVermont
Whereas, according to a research compendium that KFF, an independent health research organization, issued in
December 2025, there are stark racial disparities in the nation’s maternal and infant care health outcomes that are highly
unfavorableforBlackAmericans, and
Whereas, for example, the National Center for Chronic Disease Prevention and Health Promotion’s Division of
Reproductive Health reported that, in 2023, the pregnancy-related mortality rate per 100,000 births for Black American
womenwas49.4, whileforwhiteAmericanwomenitstoodatafar-lower14.9, and
Whereas, also in 2023, the Center for Disease Control and Prevention’s National Center for Health Statistics recorded
10.9 Black American infant deaths per 1,000 live births, and the comparable figure for White American infants was 4.5,
and
Whereas, the Black Mamas Matter Alliance (BMMA) is “an [a]lliance of perinatal, maternal, and reproductive health
Black and women-led organizations and multidisciplinary professionals … [that seeks] to build consensus and articulate
priorities…from thosedoingworkwithin… [the]communities[ofBlackAmericanwomen],”and
Whereas, among the organization’s goals are to “cultivate innovative research methods … to improve Black maternal
health … introduce and enhance holistic and comprehensive approaches to Black mamas’ care … [and] redirect and
reframetheconversationonBlackmaternalhealthandamplifythevoicesofBlackmamas,”and
Whereas, annually, BMMA designates Black Maternal Health Week, which, in 2026, is from April 11 to 17, and this
year the week’s mission is “to center the voices, leadership, and lived experiences of Black mothers while advancing
community-ledsystemschangerootedinhealing, trust, andcollaboration,”and
Whereas, coinciding with this mission is the theme, “Rooted in Justice & Joy: 10 Years of Building the Black
Maternal Health Movement,” both honoring “a decade of groundbreaking work that has transformed the landscape of
Black Maternal Health in the U.S,” and “acknowledging the persistent challenges that demand continued innovation and
action,”now therefore be it
ResolvedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentatives:
That the General Assembly recognizes the week of April 11–17, 2026, as Black Maternal Health Week in Vermont,
andbeitfurther
Resolved: ThattheSecretaryofStatebedirectedtosendacopyofthisresolutiontoBMMA.
