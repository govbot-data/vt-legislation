5 Subject: Conservation and development; water quality; lakes
6 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to create the
7 Water Quality, Lake Classification, and Antidegradation Study Group for the
8 purpose of reviewing and evaluating Vermont’s existing statutory and
9 regulatory frameworks, policies, and procedures governing water quality and
10 the classification of the waters of the State. The Study Group would
11 recommend to the General Assembly whether the current regulatory
12 framework for the waters of the State is adequate and whether legislative or
13 policy changes are needed to strengthen environmental protection and other
14 public interests in the waters of the State.
15 An act relating to water quality of the waters of Vermont

6 (c) Powers and duties. The Study Group shall:
7 (1) Develop an inventory of the existing water classification
8 designations, as set forth in the Vermont Water Quality Standards, for the
9 waters of the State, including high quality waters with water quality data that
10 meets or exceeds the minimum criteria supporting reclassification for such
11 waters.
12 (2) Assess Vermont’s obligations under the federal Clean Water Act
13 with respect to the adoption and implementation of an antidegradation policy,
14 including an evaluation of statutory and regulatory requirements and
15 identification of any legal, administrative, or practical barriers to full
16 compliance.
17 (3) Assess and identify current and potential threats to the water quality
18 of the waters of the State, as defined under the Vermont Water Quality
19 Standards. This assessment shall include an evaluation of whether existing
20 statutory and regulatory frameworks, rules, policies, and procedures are
21 adequate to protect and support the designated and existing uses of the waters

1 of the State, and whether management practices effectively minimize risks to

3 (4) Identify and evaluate the existing statutory and regulatory
4 frameworks, rules, policies, and procedures applicable to waters of the State
5 classified as Class A. This evaluation shall include a review of requirements

7 are classified as Class A waters or meet the criteria for Class A waters.
8 (5) Evaluate whether amendments to the State of Vermont’s existing
9 water classification framework are warranted. This evaluation shall
10 encompass all classes of surface waters, including streams, rivers, inland lakes,
11 and inland ponds, and shall consider whether modifications are necessary to
12 advance the public interest by promoting the ecological integrity, health, and
13 functional capacity of such waters. It shall also consider separate classification
14 systems for rivers and streams and lakes and ponds.
15 (6) Recommend legislative amendments and identify any water quality
16 rules, policies, or procedures that require revision to implement the Study
17 Group’s recommendations. Recommendations shall include aligning statutory
18 frameworks with federal and State obligations, updating regulatory standards,
19 and revising administrative practices to ensure consistency, compliance, and
20 support for improved water quality management.

1 (d) Assistance. The Study Group shall have the administrative, technical,
