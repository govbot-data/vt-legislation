2 An act relating to water quality of the waters of Vermont
3 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
4 Sec. 1. WATER QUALITY, LAKE CLASSIFICATION, AND
5 ANTIDEGRADATION STUDY GROUP; REPORT
6 (a) Creation. There is created the Water Quality, Lake Classification, and
7 Antidegradation Study Group, which shall conduct the evaluations set forth in
8 subsection (c) of this section, including the review of existing classified waters
9 of the State and candidate waters with water quality data supporting
10 reclassification, assessment of antidegradation requirements, examination of
11 the regulatory framework for Class A waters, and examination of the adequacy
12 of the current water classification system for lakes and ponds. Based on these
13 evaluations, the Study Group shall recommend to the General Assembly
14 legislative or policy changes to strengthen environmental protection, provide
15 regulatory certainty, and support public uses of State waters.
16 (b) Membership. The Study Group shall be composed of the following
17 members:
18 (1) two current members of the House of Representatives, who shall be
19 appointed by the Speaker of the House;
20 (2) two current members of the Senate, who shall be appointed by the
21 Committee on Committees;

14 (c) Powers and duties. The Study Group shall:
15 (1) Develop an inventory of the waters of the State, with the existing
16 classification designations, as set forth in the Vermont Water Quality
17 Standards, including candidate high quality waters with water quality data that
18 meets or exceeds the minimum criteria supporting reclassification for such
19 waters.
20 (2) Assess the State’s obligations under the federal Clean Water Act, 33
21 U.S.C. §§ 1251–1388, as enacted as of January 1, 2026, with respect to the

1 adoption of an antidegradation rule to implement the State’s antidegradation
2 policy under the Vermont Water Quality Standards, including an evaluation of
3 State and federal statutory and regulatory requirements and the identification
4 of any legal, administrative, policy, or practical barriers to full compliance.
5 (3) Identify and evaluate the statutory and regulatory frameworks, rules,

8 adequately protect and support designated and existing uses, and provide

10 (4) Evaluate whether the existing water classification system in the State

12 of the State’s lakes and ponds, adequately address current and potential threats
13 to the water quality of the State’s lakes and ponds, and provide regulatory
14 certainty.
15 (5) Recommend legislative amendments and identify any rules, policies,
16 or procedures that may require revision to implement the Study Group’s
17 recommendations.
18 (d) Assistance. The Study Group shall have the administrative, technical,
19 and legal assistance of the Agency of Natural Resources and shall have the
20 legal and drafting assistance of the Office of Legislative Counsel.

1 (e) Report. On or before December 15, 2026, the Study Group shall submit
2 a written report to the General Assembly that shall include its findings and
3 recommendations under subsection (c) of this section.
4 (f) Meetings.
5 (1) The Secretary of Natural Resources shall call the first meeting of the
6 Study Group to occur on or before August 1, 2026.
7 (2) The Study Group shall select at its first meeting a chair from among
8 the four legislators serving as members.
9 (3) A majority of the Study Group shall constitute a quorum.
10 (4) The Study Group shall cease to exist on February 15, 2027.
11 (g) Compensation and reimbursement.
12 (1) For attendance at meetings during adjournment of the General
13 Assembly, a legislative member of the Study Group serving in the member’s
14 capacity as a legislator shall be entitled to per diem compensation and
15 reimbursement of expenses pursuant to 2 V.S.A. § 23 for not more than eight
16 meetings. These payments shall be made from monies appropriated to the
17 General Assembly.
18 (2) Other members of the Study Group shall be entitled to per diem
19 compensation and reimbursement of expenses as permitted under 32 V.S.A.
20 § 1010 for not more than eight meetings. These payments shall be made from
21 monies appropriated to the General Assembly.
