2 An act relating to water quality of the waters of Vermont
3 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
4 Sec. 1. WATER QUALITY, LAKE CLASSIFICATION, AND
5 ANTIDEGRADATION STUDY GROUP; REPORT
6 (a) Creation. There is created the Water Quality, Lake Classification, and
7 Antidegradation Study Group, which shall conduct the evaluations set forth in
8 subsection (c) of this section, including the review of existing classified waters
9 of the State and candidate waters with water quality data supporting
10 reclassification, assessment of antidegradation requirements, examination of
11 the regulatory framework for Class A waters, and examination of the adequacy
12 of the current water classification system for lakes and ponds. Based on these
13 evaluations, the Study Group shall recommend to the General Assembly
14 legislative or policy changes to strengthen environmental protection, provide
15 regulatory certainty, and support public uses of State waters.
16 (b) Membership. The Study Group shall be composed of the following
17 members:
18 (1) three current members of the House of Representatives, not all from
19 the same political party, who shall be appointed by the Speaker of the House;
20 (2) three current members of the Senate, not all from the same political
21 party, who shall be appointed by the Committee on Committees;
22 (3) the Secretary of Natural Resources or designee;

8 Sec. 3. AGENCY OF NATURAL RESOURCES REPORT ON
9 ESTABLISHING A CERTIFICATION PROGRAM FOR
10 WETLANDS PROFESSIONALS
11 (a)(1) On or before January 15, 2027, the Secretary of Natural Resources
12 shall submit to the House Committee on Environment and the Senate
13 Committee on Natural Resources and Energy a report recommending whether
14 to establish a program to certify wetlands professionals in the State for the
15 purposes of identifying and delineating wetlands boundaries. The report shall:
16 (A) describe the benefits and disadvantages of a wetlands
17 professional certification program, including whether it could accelerate
18 wetlands permitting, reduce the amount of wetlands services available,
19 increase the cost of wetlands services, or delay the permitting process; and
20 (B) discuss how a wetlands professional certification program could
21 impact the liability of wetlands professionals, including whether certification
