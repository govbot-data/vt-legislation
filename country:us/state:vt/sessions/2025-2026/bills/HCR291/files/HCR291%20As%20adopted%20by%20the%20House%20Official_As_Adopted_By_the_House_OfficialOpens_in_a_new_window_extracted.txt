State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 291
Offered by Representative Cooper of Pownal
Offered by Senators Bongartz and Plunkett
House concurrent resolution congratulating Williams College Professor of Music W. Anthony Sheppard on
his designation as a 2026 Guggenheim Fellow
Whereas, in 1925, former Colorado U.S. Senator John Simon Guggenheim established a foundation, which
today supports “extraordinary individuals breaking new ground in the Creative Arts, Natural Sciences, Social
Sciences, Humanities, and a range of interdisciplinary fields,” and, during the past century, the John Simon
Guggenheim Memorial Foundation (the Foundation) has awarded over 19,000 scholars nearly $450 million in
fellowship grants, and
Whereas, on April 14, 2026, the Foundation introduced the 101st fellowship class, consisting of 223 new
Guggenheim Fellows, selected from nearly 5,000 applicants who are engaged in 55 disciplines; who represent
97 academic institutions; and who reside in the United States, Canada, and eight other nations, and
Whereas, one of the 2026 Guggenheim Fellows is Pownal resident W. Anthony Sheppard, the Marylin and
Arthur Levitt Professor of Music at Williams College, a faculty member since 1996, a graduate of Amherst
College, and holder of a master’s degree and a doctoral degree from Princeton University, and
Whereas, Professor W. Anthony Sheppard’s research and teaching interests range from American musical
history to Asian (especially Japanese) music, Broadway musical theater, cross-cultural influences, exoticism,
globalization’s impact on music, film music analysis, opera, popular music, and vocal timbre, and
Whereas, while on the Williams College faculty, he has edited and contributed to volumes on the bandleader
Paul Whiteman and the Williams College alumnus, Broadway composer, and lyricist Stephen Sondheim; he has
written two books, one exploring Japan’s influence on American music and a second examining exotic
influences in modernist musical theater; he has published many thoughtful articles; he has edited leading
academic music journals; and he has been the recipient of numerous accolades and honors; and he is a popular
lecturer at musical events, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly congratulates Williams College Professor of Music W. Anthony Sheppard on his
designation as a 2026 Guggenheim Fellow, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Professor W. Anthony
Sheppard.
