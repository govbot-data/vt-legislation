Act No. 119 (H.588). An act relating to professions and occupations regulated by the Office
of Professional Regulation
Subjects: Professions and occupations
This act makes miscellaneous changes to laws pertaining to various regulated
professions and occupations. Specifically, it explicitly enables the Office of Professional
Regulation to rescind licenses in instances of administrative error, nonpayment, or loss of
compact license or privilege. It enables the Office of Professional Regulation to enforce
against attempted fraudulent or deceptive procurement or use of a license. It requires
professional regulation board members to be adults. It corrects terminology for license
denials of foreign applicants. It creates additional combinations of education and
experience as prerequisites for accountant licensure. It creates a limited academic dentist
license. It removes the requirement for advanced practice registered nurse (APRN)
license renewal to include documentation of completion of the APRN practice
requirement. It enables pharmacists to prescribe, order, or administer tests for COVID-
19, influenza, and streptococcal pharyngitis and subsequent drug treatment, and to
prescribe or order medications for the prevention of human immunodeficiency virus,
including those for pre-exposure and post-exposure prophylaxis. It enables the Office of
Professional Regulation to create temporary policies to supplement psychologist
licensure educational requirements notwithstanding any contrary rules. It eliminates the
advisory committee on midwifery that reports to the Office of Professional Regulation
and the Commissioner of Health and changes the license renewal requirement for
midwives to submit individual practice data to a discretionary decision to be made by the
Director of Professional Regulation. It modifies the definition of “practice of funeral
service” to include disposition of human remains by cremation, alkaline hydrolysis, or
natural organic reduction. It requires the Office of Professional Regulation to report on
the possible professional regulation of speech-language pathology assistants. It requires
the registration of massage “establishments,” creates new fees for these establishments,
and expands on operational requirements and related unprofessional conduct. It also
enables the Board of Medical Practice to conduct criminal background checks for certain
professions in a manner similar to the Office of Professional Regulation.
Effective Dates: Multiple effective dates, beginning on June 8, 2026
