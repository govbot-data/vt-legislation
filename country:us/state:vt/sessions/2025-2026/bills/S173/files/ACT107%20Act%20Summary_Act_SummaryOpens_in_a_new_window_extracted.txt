Act No. 107 (S.173). An act relating to vocational rehabilitation and apprenticeships
Subjects: Labor; State Employees Labor Relations Act; Workers’ Compensation;
vocational rehabilitation; Vermont Labor Relations Board; mediator
This act requires that the Department of Labor form explaining an injured worker’s
rights include the worker’s right to request vocational rehabilitation services in the future
if the work injury affects the worker’s ability to earn the worker’s preinjury wage. This
act creates the Vocational Rehabilitation Working Group to provide recommendations to
the General Assembly on how to improve the current vocational rehabilitation system to
ensure that it meets the needs of eligible injured workers in a timely and cost-effective
manner. The act identifies several topics for consideration by the Working Group,
including the statutorily required initial pre-screening requirement, vocational
rehabilitation generally, and wage replacement benefits for injured workers. The
Working Group is directed to provide a written report to the committees of jurisdiction on
or before December 15, 2026. Finally, this act provides updates and technical corrections
to Vermont’s apprenticeship programs, including changes consistent with federal
regulations and the expansion of program access to underrepresented communities.
Effective Date: July 1, 2026
