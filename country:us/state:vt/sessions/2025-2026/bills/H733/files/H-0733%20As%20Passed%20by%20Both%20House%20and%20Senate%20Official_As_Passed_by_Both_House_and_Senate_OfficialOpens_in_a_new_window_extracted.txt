4 A franchisee may terminate a franchise agreement without penalty or fees,

9 § 4056. TRANSFER OR SALE OF BUSINESS BY FRANCHISEE
10 (a) Death of franchisee. No franchisor shall deny the surviving spouse,
11 heirs, or estate of a deceased franchisee the opportunity to participate in the
12 ownership of the franchise under a valid franchise agreement for not less than
13 12 months after the death of the franchisee or majority shareholder of the
14 franchisee. During that time, the surviving spouse, heirs, or estate of the
15 deceased shall either satisfy all of the then-current qualifications for a
16 purchaser of a franchised business or sell, transfer, or assign the franchise to a
17 person that satisfies the franchisor’s then-current standards for new
18 franchisees. The rights granted pursuant to this section shall be granted
19 subject to the surviving spouse, heirs, or estate of the deceased maintaining all
20 standards and obligations of the franchise agreement.

11 § 4058. FRANCHISE AGREEMENT PROVISIONS
12 (a) Pricing. A franchisor shall not require a franchisee to sell any product
13 or service for a price at a loss or otherwise not reasonably acceptable to the
14 franchisee.
15 (b) Liquidated damages. Liquidated damages clauses shall not be
16 enforceable in a case of termination of the franchise agreement by the
17 franchisor.
18 (c) No cross default. A default under one franchise agreement shall not in
19 and of itself constitute a default under another franchise agreement to which
20 the franchisee or an affiliate of the franchisee is a party.

1 (d) Control. No franchisor shall maintain direct or indirect control of the
2 franchisee’s employees or of the day-to-day operation of the franchised
3 business.
4 (e) Hours of operation. No renewal of a franchise agreement or change in
5 the operations manual or like policy shall impose a change in the hours of
6 operation in which the franchisee deems that it is unprofitable or unsafe to
7 operate, or that interferes with a religious holiday.
8 (f) Notices. All notices of termination or nonrenewal required by this
9 chapter:
10 (1) shall be in writing;
11 (2) shall be posted by registered, certified, or other receipted mail;
12 delivered by telegram; or personally delivered to the franchisee; and
13 (3) shall contain a statement of intent to terminate or not renew the
14 franchised business together with:
15 (A) the reasons therefor; and
16 (B) the effective date of such termination or nonrenewal or
17 expiration.
18 (g) Negotiated franchise agreement. Any franchise agreement that differs
19 from the disclosed franchise agreement shall, in totality, have negotiated terms
20 that are to the benefit of the franchisee.

1 (h) Arbitration. Pre-dispute, mandatory arbitration clauses are forbidden in

3 (i) Venue of disputes. A provision in a franchise agreement restricting
