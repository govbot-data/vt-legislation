House concurrent resolution celebrating the importance of the Vermont visitor economy and recognizing
April 16, 2026, as Tourism Economy Day at the State House
Whereas, Vermont is a world-class destination for outdoor recreation, dining, lodging, art, and history that
attracts visitors and new residents, incentivizes Vermonters to stay, and creates business opportunities, and
Whereas, Vermont’s visitor economy has a $4.2 billion economic impact and employs more than 10 percent
of Vermont’s workforce, and
Whereas, in 2024, 16 million people visited Vermont, contributing an estimated $293.5 million dollars in
State and local taxes, and
Whereas, tourism and hospitality are two of the largest industries in Vermont, creating 31,780 jobs, and
Whereas, tourism and hospitality are vital to the sustainability of rural and urban communities alike, across
all 14 counties, and
Whereas, meaningful representation and inclusion are essential to ensure Vermont is welcoming to
individuals from all backgrounds so that they can experience the joy of visiting the State, and
Whereas, industry leaders who prioritize community values in their work and promote destination
stewardship benefit Vermonters and the State’s economy, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly celebrates the importance of the Vermont visitor economy and recognizes April
16, 2026, as Tourism Economy Day at the State House, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to the Vermont Chamber
of Commerce and Ski Vermont.
