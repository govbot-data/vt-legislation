Act No. 179 (S.328). An act relating to housing and common interest communities
Subjects: Housing; Executive; Secretary of State; common interest communities;
Agency of Human Services; Service-Supported Housing Advisory Council;
conservation and development; Office of the State Treasurer; Vermont Economic
Development Authority; Vermont Housing Finance Agency; Rental Housing
Revolving Loan Program; Department of Housing and Community Development;
Vermont Rental Housing Improvement Program; municipal and county
government; special assessments; municipal planning; municipal zoning; State
Community Investment Program; Vermont Housing and Conservation Board
This act includes miscellaneous housing provisions enacted by the General Assembly.
The act requires the Secretary of State to provide the public with information about
Vermont’s common interest communities.
The act creates the Service-Supported Housing Advisory Council within the
Department of Disabilities, Aging, and Independent Living for the purpose of identifying
opportunities for increased alignment between human services programs and policies
serving individuals who receive Medicaid-funded Developmental Disability Services and
housing capital and support services programs.
The act increases the credit facility within the Office of the State Treasurer from 10 to
12.5 percent of the State’s average cash balance and authorizes the Office of the State
Treasurer to develop and administer an Off-Site Construction Accelerator Pilot Program
to explore the possibility of reducing housing development costs through modular
construction.
The act amends the definition of “eligible facility” or “eligible project” under the
statutes governing the Vermont Economic Development Authority to authorize the
Authority to finance multiunit housing developments of five or more units when certain
conditions are met. The Authority shall defer to the Vermont Housing Finance Agency
(VHFA) on projects and shall not fund projects being primarily developed for occupancy
by persons and families of low or moderate income or projects utilizing funds from
VHFA.
The act codifies the Rental Housing Revolving Loan Program within VHFA
statutes and amends the Program to authorize funding for housing serving low-income
individuals by allowing projects to serve individuals earning up to 80 or up to
150 percent of area median income as outlined in the Program requirements.
The act authorizes municipalities, upon approval of the legislative body of the
municipality and subject to a minimum marketability safeguard, to issue revenue bonds
backed solely by special assessments for the same purpose for which the special
assessments are levied. The act also amends the chapter of Title 24 on indebtedness to

ensure that special assessments securing a special assessment bond are not treated as
property taxes subject to retention for purposes of tax increment financing.
The act requires the housing element of a municipal plan to include an analysis of any
regulatory, labor, and physical constraints preventing the development, redevelopment, or
rehabilitation of sufficient housing to meet the housing needs and targets of the
municipality and requires a description of what actions the municipality may take to
accommodate the projected housing needs, including progress made towards construction
of the housing units identified as needed to meet projected housing targets.
The act amends municipal zoning provisions to require municipal bylaws to allow
mobile, modular, manufactured, and prefabricated housing in any district that allows
year-round residential development, to remove authority for municipal bylaws to require
more than four dwelling units in a multiunit dwelling served by municipal sewer and
water on the same lot size as a single-unit dwelling, and prohibits municipal bylaws from
requiring a duplex to be constructed on an owner-occupied lot. The removal of the
authority for municipalities to require more than four units in a multiunit dwelling served
by municipal water and sewer and the new prohibition on municipal bylaws requiring a
duplex to be constructed on an owner-occupied lot are repealed on January 1, 2028.
The act amends the State Community Investment Program to allow access to the
Program for downtown and village centers that are not recognized by the National
Register of Historic Places if they are preexisting new town centers.
The act amends the statutes governing the State Treasurer credit facility annual report;
the VHFA Rental Housing Revolving Loan Program annual report; and the Vermont
Rental Housing Improvement Program annual report.
Finally, the act requires the Service-Supported Housing Advisory Council to report
annually by November 15; the State Treasurer to report by January 15, 2027, regarding
the Off-Site Construction Accelerator Pilot Program; the Office of Legislative Counsel to
report on common interest communities by November 15, 2026; the Vermont Housing
and Conservation Board to report by January 15, 2027, on progress made toward meeting
the goals identified in the Farmworker Housing Needs Assessment of 2021; and the
Department of Housing and Community Development to report on the corporate
purchase of single- and two-family homes in Vermont by November 15, 2026.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on July 1, 2026
