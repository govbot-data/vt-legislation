14 An act relating to the provision of menstrual products in postsecondary
15 schools located in Vermont
16 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
17 Sec. 1. 16 V.S.A. § 1432 is amended to read:
18 § 1432. MENSTRUAL PRODUCTS
19 (a) By enacting this statute, the General Assembly intends to ensure that all
20 students attending a public school, or an approved independent school, or a

1 postsecondary school located in Vermont have access to menstrual products at
2 no cost and without having to request them.
3 (b)(1) A school district, and an approved independent school, and a
4 postsecondary school located in Vermont shall make menstrual products
5 available at no cost for each school within the district or under the jurisdiction
6 of the board of the independent school or postsecondary school in:
7 (A) a majority of gender-neutral bathrooms and bathrooms
8 designated for female students that are generally used by students who are
9 eight years of age or older; and
10 (B) the school nurse’s office or school-operated health center.
11 (2) The school district, or independent school, or postsecondary school,
12 in consultation with the school nurse or other medical professional who
13 provides services to the school, shall determine which of the gender-neutral
14 bathrooms and bathrooms designated for female students to stock with
15 menstrual products and which brands to use.
16 (c) School districts, and approved independent schools, and postsecondary
17 schools located in Vermont shall bear the cost of supplying menstrual products
18 and may seek grants or partner with a nonprofit or community-based
19 organization to fulfill this obligation.
20 Sec. 2. EFFECTIVE DATE
21 This act shall take effect on July 1, 2026.
