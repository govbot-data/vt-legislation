Act No. 131 (H.567). An act relating to unclaimed property, State retirement systems, and
capital debt
Subjects: Property; unclaimed property; decedents’ estates; Executive Branch;
education; retirement; State Employees’ Retirement System; State Teachers’
Retirement System; Municipal Employees’ Retirement System; postemployment
benefits; capital debt
This act makes changes to the statutes governing unclaimed property, State retirement
systems, and the Capital Debt Affordability Advisory Committee annual report.
Secs. 1–5 make amendments to the statutes governing unclaimed property. The act
allows the State Treasurer to accept unclaimed property under a medical insurance policy
and authorizes the Treasurer to require an explanation of benefits when unclaimed
property is submitted to the Treasurer’s office. The act increases the monetary thresholds
in numerous instances to allow the Treasurer to issue expedited unclaimed property
claims. Lastly, relating to unclaimed property, the act increases the threshold for
unclaimed property that is greater than 10 years old that is withheld from the General
Fund from $100.00 to $150.00. The act requires these monies to be transferred to the
Vermont Retirement Security Fund up to a total annual maximum of $300,000.00. The
act provides the Treasurer with the sole discretion of transferring some of the monies to
the Vermont Higher Education Endowment Trust Fund, still limited by the annual cap of
$300,000.00. On January 1, 2040, the statute is amended to deposit all unclaimed
property that is at least 10 years old and valued at $150.00 or more into the Vermont
Higher Education Endowment Trust Fund up to an annual maximum of $300,000.00.
Secs. 6–22 are related to the Vermont State Retirement Systems. Sec. 6 authorizes the
assessment of penalties against any political subdivision of the State participating in the
State Retirement System for payments due that are not received within 30 days after the
installment due date set by the Retirement Board. Sec. 7 creates the Pension and Benefits
Funding Task Force to review and report on the funding methodologies of the Vermont
State Retirement System and the State Teachers’ Retirement System, with a report due on
or before December 15, 2026, regarding findings and recommendations for legislative
action. Secs. 8–20 move the administrative and fiduciary oversight responsibilities for
the State Employees’ Postemployment Benefits Trust Fund and the Retired Teachers’
Health and Medical Benefits Fund to the Vermont Pension Investment Commission.
Secs. 21 and 22 authorize a member of the State Teachers’ Retirement System or the
Municipal Employees’ Retirement System who is elected to serve as the president of an
employee organization designated as the exclusive bargaining representative to receive
creditable service for the period served as president provided that the employee
organization deposits in the pension fund on behalf of the member the statutorily required
amount.
