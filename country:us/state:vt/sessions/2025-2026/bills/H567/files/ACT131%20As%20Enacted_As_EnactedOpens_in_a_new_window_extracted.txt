Sec. 7. PENSION AND BENEFITS FUNDING TASK FORCE; REPORT
(a) Creation. There is created the Pension and Benefits Funding Task
Force to review and report on the funding methodologies of the Vermont State
Retirement System and the State Teachers’ Retirement System of Vermont,
including the State Employees’ Postemployment Benefits Trust Fund and the
Retired Teachers’ Health and Medical Benefits Fund.
(b) Membership. The Task Force shall be composed of the following
members:
(1) the Chair of the House Committee on Appropriations, who shall be
an ex officio member;
(2) the Chair of the Senate Committee on Appropriations, who shall be
an ex officio member;
(3) the Secretary of Administration or designee;
(4) the State Treasurer or designee;
(5) one member, appointed by the Vermont National Education
Association;
(6) one member, appointed by the Vermont State Employees’
Association; and
VT LEG #390203 v.1

No. 131 Page 8 of 34
2026
(7) one member, appointed by the President of the Vermont Troopers’
Association.
(c) Powers and duties.
(1) The Task Force shall review the existing funding schedules and
methodologies for the following retirement systems:
(A) the Vermont State Retirement Fund, 3 V.S.A. § 473;
(B) the State Employees’ Postemployment Benefits Trust Fund,
3 V.S.A. § 479a;
(C) the Vermont Teachers’ Retirement Fund, 16 V.S.A. § 1944; and
(D) the Retired Teachers’ Health and Medical Benefits Fund,
16 V.S.A. § 1944b.
(2) Following the review required by subdivision (1) of this subsection,
the Task Force shall make recommendations about the funding methodologies
of these systems, consistent with actuarial and governmental accounting
standards, with due consideration of the following:
(A) the long-term sustainability of the pension and benefit plans;
(B) the total cost of funding the systems over time;
(C) the predictability and stability of annual actuarially determined
employer contributions;
(D) the impact of any change in funding methodologies on the State’s
credit rating; and

2026
(E) best funding practices as recommended by actuaries and
implemented by peer public pension systems.
(3) The Task Force shall not make recommendations on member
benefits, contribution levels, or assumed rates of return.
(d) Assistance.
(1) The Task Force shall have the administrative, technical, legal, and
fiscal assistance of the Office of the State Treasurer, Vermont Pension
Investment Commission, and the Joint Fiscal Office.
(2) The Office of the State Treasurer is authorized to contract for
advisory services for the Task Force from an actuarial and financial expert, as
necessary.
(e) Report. On or before December 15, 2026, the Task Force shall submit a
final report to the General Assembly, the Retirement Board of the Vermont
State Retirement System, and the Board of Trustees of the State Teachers’
Retirement System of Vermont with its findings and any recommendations for
legislative action.
(f) Meetings.
(1) The State Treasurer or designee shall be the chair. The Task Force
shall elect a vice chair from among its members at the first meeting.
(2) The Chair shall call the first meeting of the Task Force to occur on
or before June 15, 2026.
(3) A majority of the membership shall constitute a quorum.

capacity as a legislator shall be entitled to per diem compensation and
reimbursement of expenses pursuant to 2 V.S.A. § 23 for not more than six
meetings. These payments shall be made from monies appropriated to the
General Assembly.
