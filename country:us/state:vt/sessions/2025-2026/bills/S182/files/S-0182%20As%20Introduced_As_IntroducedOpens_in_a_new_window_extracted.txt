5 Subject: Criminal procedures; animal cruelty; forfeiture
6 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to expedite the
7 procedures for forfeiture of animals that are subject to seizure as a result of
8 animal cruelty violations and to expand the use of the Animal Welfare Fund to
9 provide reimbursement for the costs of caring for seized animals.
10 An act relating to animal cruelty violation forfeitures and the Animal
11 Welfare Fund

1 (1) Voluntary surrender. A humane officer may accept animals

16 situation in which the humane officer determines that an animal’s life is in

18 safety, the officer may seize the animal without a warrant. The humane officer
19 shall immediately take an animal seized under this subdivision to a licensed
20 veterinarian for medical attention to stabilize the animal’s condition and to
21 assess the health of the animal.
