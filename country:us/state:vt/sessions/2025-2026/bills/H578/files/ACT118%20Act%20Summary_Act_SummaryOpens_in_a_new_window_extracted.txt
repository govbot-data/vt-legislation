Act No. 118 (H.578). An act relating to penalties and procedures for animal cruelty
offenses
Subjects: Crimes; cruelty to animals; humane and proper treatment of animals
This act expands the conduct that constitutes animal cruelty to include a broader
definition of sexual conduct with an animal; knowingly possessing, filming, or
distributing obscene visual images of sexual conduct with an animal; violating a court
order not to possess, own, care for, reside with, have custody of, or work with animal;
and knowingly refusing to comply with a court order to permit periodic unannounced
visits by a humane officer. The act further expands the conduct that constitutes
aggravated animal cruelty to include any sexual conduct with an animal when the
conduct involves a minor. The act expands the sanctions a sentencing court may impose
on a person convicted of animal cruelty and makes certain sanctions mandatory.
The act establishes deadlines for proceedings to permit civil forfeiture of an animal
subject to cruelty and requires owners seeking to challenge a seizure to post security at
the time of seizure to pay for the costs of care, which a court may reduce or waive on the
basis of financial hardship. The act provides immunity to humane officers, animal
shelters, and rescue organizations for actions taken in good faith reliance on forfeiture
law, not including gross negligence or intentional misconduct. The act requires the
Division of Animal Welfare to adopt rules for the receipt, management, and distribution
of the security payments; authorizes the Animal Welfare Fund to accept and hold security
payments; and authorizes the Director of Animal Welfare to make distributions and
reimbursements from the Fund. The act requires the Director of Animal Welfare to
propose to the General Assembly security amounts and payment schedules. The act sets
a security amount for nonlivestock animals and livestock animals until legislation
establishing the amount of the security and the payment schedule takes effect.
Effective Date: July 1, 2026
