State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 271
Offered by Representatives Bartley of Fairfax, Austin of Colchester, Bishop of Colchester, Critchlow of
Colchester, and Pezzo of Colchester
House concurrent resolution congratulating the 2026 Colchester High School State championship debate
team
Whereas, successful debaters must thoroughly research the topic, develop a comprehensive command of the
facts, strategically and carefully insert a tad of witticism at the appropriate moment, and always respond to the
opponent’s contradicting arguments, confidently but not antagonistically, and
Whereas, this year’s Vermont State debate championship was held on Saturday, March 14, 2026, at
Champlain Valley Union High School, and the eager student debaters were assigned the topic, “Should the
federal government ban corporations from buying single-family homes?”, and
Whereas, the students representing Colchester High School at this culminating event in the school year’s
debate schedule were the team’s able cocaptains, Ariana Riedinger and Naomi Shpaner, who consistently
demonstrated why they were selected for these positions, and
Whereas, Colchester’s solid performance earlier in the day earned these two polished debaters a berth in the
semifinal round against Hanover High School in New Hampshire (which regularly competes against Vermont
teams), and the Colchester students’ informative and, most importantly, persuasive arguments won the judges’
approval for entry into the decisive final round against Montpelier, and
Whereas, the four experienced students’ verbal exchanges reflected their agility as public forum debaters, but
after careful deliberation, the three judges voted two to one to award Colchester the championship’s top
honor, and
Whereas, throughout the academic year, Ariana Riedinger and Naomi Shpaner were the leaders of an
outstanding team of Colchester debaters, who were Lunar Wager, Isabella Rodriguez, McCartney Holtz, Brin
Averett, Josh Bierer, Ethan Thompson, Norah Glass, Isla Stowell, Alyssa Bierer, and Leah Gilmond, and the
team’s superb faculty advisor, Oliver Kranichfeld, offered guidance and wisdom that greatly contributed to
Colchester’s well-deserved State championship victory, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly congratulates the 2026 Colchester High School State championship debate team,
and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Colchester High
School.
