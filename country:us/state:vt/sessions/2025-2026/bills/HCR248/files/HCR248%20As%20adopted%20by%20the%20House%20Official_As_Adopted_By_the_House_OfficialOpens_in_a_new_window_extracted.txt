State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 248
Offered by Representative Southworth of Walden
House concurrent resolution congratulating the 2026 Hazen Union High School Wildcats Division III
championship boys’ basketball team
Whereas, the Hazen boys’ basketball team has established an enviable record on the court, appearing
in each of the most recent five championship games at the Barre Auditorium, but despite great efforts, it
had not brought home the title in 2023, 2024, or 2025, so in 2026, the Wildcats were determined to end
the season triumphantly, and
Whereas, in the decisive contest, the second-seeded Wildcats were paired against the top-ranked
Randolph Galloping Ghosts, challenging the Hazen hoopsters to prove themselves worthy of this
athletic honor, and
Whereas, although the Galloping Ghosts dominated early on, building a 28–26 halftime lead, the
Wildcats signaled their readiness to surge ahead, closing the half with an 8–2 scoring advantage, and
once the teams resumed play in the third quarter, Hazen ignited a powerful offensive charge, finishing
the contest with a convincing 66–49 victory, delighting their exuberant fans, and
Whereas, the pleased Wildcats (20–4) were Jeter Demers, Kobe Smith, Noah Foster, Jameson
Lamarre, Talon Michaud, Ethan Gann, Sullivan Laflam, Morgan Michaud, Dawson Michaud, Lincoln
Hill, Lucian Jurkiewicz, and Chayse Newell, and
Whereas, Head Coach Aaron Hill and assistant coaches Adam Gann, Travis Hill, Kevin Doyon, Letty
Hill, and Evans Bouchard motivated the Wildcats to perform their best, and team managers Blair
Demers and Zander Dimick were always supportive of their classmates, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly congratulates the 2026 Hazen Union High School Wildcats Division III
championship boys’ basketball team, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Hazen Union
High School.
