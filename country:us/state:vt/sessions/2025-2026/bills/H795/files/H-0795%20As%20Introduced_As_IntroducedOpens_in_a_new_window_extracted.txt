5 § 4374. EMERGENCY PLAN
6 (a) A children’s camp shall develop and implement an emergency plan

8 (1) specifies muster zones for campers and camp staff to gather during

11 (2) establishes procedures for responding to an emergency event,
12 including:
13 (A) a lost camper;
14 (B) a fire on the children’s camp premises;
15 (C) a severe injury, severe illness, serious accident, or death of one or
16 more campers, visitors, camp staff, or camp volunteers that occurs on the
17 children’s camp premises or while under the supervision of camp staff;
18 (D) an aquatic emergency if the children’s camp borders a
19 watercourse, lake, pond, or any other body of water;

1 § 4375. FLOOD HAZARD AREAS; PARENTAL NOTIFICATION
2 If any area of a children’s camp is located within a flood hazard area or
3 mapped river corridor, the children’s camp shall notify the parent or legal
4 guardian of each current and prospective camper in writing. The children’s
5 camp shall obtain a written statement acknowledging receipt of this notice
6 from each camper’s parent or guardian prior to the start of the camper’s
7 attendance at the children’s camp.
8 § 4376. TRAINING AND SAFETY ORIENTATION
9 (a) Each year prior to the arrival of campers, a children’s camp shall:
10 (1) ensure each staff member and volunteer successfully completes a
11 training on the children’s camp’s approved emergency plan in compliance with
12 any minimum standards required by the Department by rule;
13 (2) instruct each staff member and volunteer on the proper procedures to
14 follow in an emergency event under the approved emergency plan; and
15 (3) maintain written records documenting each staff member’s and
16 volunteer’s participation in and successful completion of the training required
17 pursuant to this subsection.
18 (b) Within the first 48 hours of the start of any children’s camp session, a
19 camp staff member shall conduct a mandatory health and safety orientation
20 that:

9 § 4377. COMPLAINT AND INSPECTION PROCESS
10 (a)(1) The Department shall maintain on its website a publicly accessible
11 complaint page for use by any individual, including a camper, parent or legal
12 guardian, camp staff member, or volunteer, who wishes to report that a
13 children’s camp is noncompliant with this chapter.
14 (2) Each licensed children’s camp shall maintain a link to the
15 Department’s complaint page in a prominent location on its website.
16 (b) The Department shall investigate each complaint filed with the
17 Department using the publicly accessible web page or by other means to
18 ensure a children’s camp is properly implementing its approved emergency
19 plan and other obligations under this chapter. Each investigation shall include
20 an in-person inspection of the children’s camp in question.
