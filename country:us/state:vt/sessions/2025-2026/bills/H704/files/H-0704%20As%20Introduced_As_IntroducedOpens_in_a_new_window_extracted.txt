17 Sec. 44. TENANT REPRESENTATION PILOT PROGRAM
18 (a) Creation; purpose. Vermont Legal Aid shall create and administer a
19 two-year Tenant Representation Pilot Program:

4 (2) to determine the impact of representation on the issuance of writs of

6 (b) Tenant eligibility. Vermont Legal Aid may enter a notice of appearance
7 on behalf of a residential tenant in Lamoille or Windsor County who is served
8 with a summons and complaint in an ejectment action, consents to the
9 representation, and meets the following criteria:
10 (1) household income equals or is less than 120 percent of State area
11 median income;
12 (2) the cost of rent equals or exceeds 30 percent of household income; or
13 (3) household expenses exceed income.
14 (c) Scope of representation.
15 (1) Full representation through the Program is limited to eviction.
16 (2) The pursuit of counterclaims shall be at the discretion of appointed
17 counsel.
18 (d) Conflicts of interest.
19 (1) Vermont Legal Aid may subcontract to Legal Services Vermont if it
20 is unable to provide tenant representation due to a conflict of interest as
21 defined by the Vermont Rules of Professional Conduct.
