An act relating to the excess spending threshold
14 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
15 Sec. 1. ALLOWABLE GROWTH IN EDUCATION SPENDING FOR
16 FISCALYEARS 2028 AND 2029
17 (a) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, for fiscal years 2028 and
18 2029 only, a school district’s per pupil education spending, as defined in
19 16 V.S.A. § 4001(14), shall not exceed the sum of the district’s per pupil
20 education spending in the prior fiscal year and the district’s allowable growth.
21 (b) As used in this section:

1 (1) “Allowable growth” for a school district means the product of the
2 district’s allowable growth percentage and the district’s per pupil education
3 spending in the prior fiscal year.
4 (2)(A) “Allowable growth percentage” for a school district means nine
5 percent of the difference in per pupil education spending in the prior fiscal
6 year between the district and the highest spending district, excluding gores,
7 expressed as a percentage of the district’s per pupil education spending in the
8 prior fiscal year, provided that the minimum allowable growth percentage shall
9 be three percent.
10 (B) A school district’s “allowable growth percentage” is calculated as
11 follows:
12 (i) subtract the district’s per pupil education spending in the prior
13 fiscal year from the highest per pupil education spending in any district,
14 excluding gores, in the prior fiscal year;
15 (ii) divide the difference determined under subdivision (i) of this
16 subdivision (2)(B) by the district’s per pupil education spending in the prior
17 fiscal year; and
18 (iii) multiply the percentage determined under subdivision (ii) of
19 this subdivision (2)(B) by nine percent.
20 Sec.2. EFFECTIVE DATE
21 This act shall take effect on July 1, 2026.

(C) A school district’s excess spending shall be zero if any of the
following conditions is met:
(i) the district’s education spending is not greater than the
district’s educating spending for the preceding school year;
(ii) the district’s per pupil education spending is not greater than
the district’s per pupil education spending for the preceding school year; or
(iii) the Secretary of Education, with the advice of three business
managers and three superintendents selected by the Secretary, determines that
the increase in the district’s per pupil education spending above the excess
spending threshold was for good cause or beyond the district’s control, such as
