House concurrent resolution honoring Professor Emeritus Willard Sterne Randall for his significant contributions to
journalism andtothescholarlyadvancementofAmericanhistoricalbiography
Whereas, Willard Randall has enlightened and informed readers, both as a journalist and later as a prolific scholar of
earlyAmericanpoliticalandmilitaryhistory, and
Whereas, his professional life started as a feature writer and investigative journalist, whose articles appeared in the
Philadelphia Bulletin, The Philadelphia Inquirer magazine, Philadelphia Magazine, and through the Time-Life News
Service, and
Whereas, reflective of his outstanding journalistic oeuvre, he was the recipient of the Columbia Graduate School of
Journalism’s National Magazine Award for public service, the Standard Gravure Corporation award, the Hillman Prize,
theGeraldLoebjournalism award, andtheJohnHancockjournalism award, and
Whereas, after 17 years pounding the reporter’s pavement, Willard Randall entered the scholarly ranks, and he
prepared for his transformed career by completing advanced studies at Princeton University, culminating in a master’s
degreeinAmericanhistory, and
Whereas, Willard Randall wasa respected facultymember in American historyat John CabotUniversity in Rome, the
University of Vermont, and Champlain College, where he was a distinguished scholar in history and is now professor
emeritus, and
Whereas, he wrote revelatory and award-winning biographies examining our State’s and nation’s early military and
political leadership, including biographies of Ethan Allen, Benedict Arnold, Benjamin and William Franklin, Alexander
Hamilton, JohnHancock, ThomasJefferson, andGeorgeWashington, and
Whereas, Willard Randall is a contributing editor to MHQ: The Quarterly Journal of Military History, and among his
academic honors are the Award of Merit of the American Revolution Round Table and the Thomas Fleming Award for
OutstandingMilitaryHistoryWriting, highlightinghisroleasaneminentAmericanhistoryscholar, now therefore be it
ResolvedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentatives:
That the General Assembly honors Professor Emeritus Willard Sterne Randall for his significant contributions to
journalism andthescholarlyadvancementofAmericanhistoricalbiography, andbeitfurther
Resolved: ThattheSecretaryofStatebedirectedtosendacopyofthisresolutiontoWillardRandall.
