House concurrent resolution celebrating the 75th anniversary of Marlboro Music, a premier chamber music
educationalinstitution, performancevenue, andincubator
Whereas, American chamber music and Marlboro are synonymous, and the musical inspiration and nurturing
influenceofthisvenerableVermontartisticinstitutiononmusicperformanceandeducationareimmense, and
Whereas, the establishment of Marlboro Music represented the epitome of the American immigrant experience, as, in
1951, members of the extraordinarily musically talented Busch and Moyse families, namely cellist Hermann Busch; his
brother, violinistAdolf Busch;his son-in-law, pianist RudolphSerkin, who, for halfa century, would serveas Marlboro’s
esteemed music director; flutist brothers Marcel Moyse and Louis Moyse; and Louis’s then-wife, violinist Blanche
Moyse, created this organization on the campus of Marlboro College (now known as the Marlboro Institute for Liberal
ArtsandInterdisciplinaryStudies), and
Whereas, during the annual summer musicians’ residency program, aspiring professionals refine their playing
technique and musical connoisseurship under the expert tutelage of musical luminaries, and, later in the summer, they
jointlyperform inmagnificentconcerts, and
Whereas, during the 1964 residency program, several Marlboro attendees established the famous Guarneri Quartet,
and, at the time, the Musical AmericaInternational Directory of thePerforming Arts listed113 American chamber music
ensembles, and, in 2026, that number has happily risen to approximately 650, many of which proudly include Marlboro
alumni, and
Whereas, cellist and conductor Pablo Casals; violinist Felix Galimir; cellist Madeline Foley; and composers Samuel
Barber, ElliottCarter, andAaronCoplandareamongthemusicianswhohavecoachedatMarlboroMusic, and
Whereas, many Vermonters, trustees, staff, musicians, and community partners have worked diligently to sustain
MarlboroMusic, whichisanimportantcontributortoVermont’screativeeconomy, and
Whereas, when Marlboro College closed, the future of Marlboro Music’s picturesque Potash Hill campus was
uncertain, and, after complex negotiations, the campus’s long-term continuity was ensured when Marlboro Music,
through a newly formed associated organization, Potash Hill, Inc., assumed ownership, and now the famed pianists
MitsukoUchidaandJonathanBissarecodirectors, Miles Cohenisartisticadministrator, andKalyaYannatosis managing
director, now therefore be it
ResolvedbytheSenateandHouseofRepresentatives:
That the General Assembly celebrates the 75th anniversary of Marlboro Music, a premier chamber music educational
institution, performancevenue, andincubator, andbeitfurther
