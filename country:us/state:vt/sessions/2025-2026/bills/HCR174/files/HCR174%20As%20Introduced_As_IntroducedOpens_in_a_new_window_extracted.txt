State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 174
Offered by Representatives Rachelson of Burlington and Chapin of East Montpelier
House concurrent resolution recognizing January 27, 2026, as International Holocaust Remembrance Day in
Vermont
Whereas, January 27 is observed annually as International Holocaust Remembrance Day, to commemorate
the victims of the Holocaust and to ensure that future generations understand a recurrence must be
prevented, and
Whereas, the Holocaust, also known as the Shoah, was the systematic state-sponsored persecution and
murder of approximately six million Jews by Nazi Germany and its collaborators during World War II; and
millions of others, including Roma, Poles, persons with disabilities, LGBTQ+ individuals, Soviet prisoners of
war, and political dissidents were also targeted, and
Whereas, the date for International Holocaust Remembrance Day was selected because it marks the January
27, 1945, anniversary of the liberation of the Auschwitz Concentration Camp, where the Nazis murdered at
least 1.5 million men, women, and children, and
Whereas, the memory of the Holocaust serves as a stark reminder of the devastating consequences of
intolerance and bigotry and a warning that, if not remembered, the same can occur again, and
Whereas, antisemitism, including harassment on the basis of actual or perceived Jewish origin, ancestry,
ethnicity, identity, affiliation, or faith, remains a disturbingly persistent and pervasive problem, and
Whereas, survivors of the Holocaust, their families and communities, and the organizations, such as the
Vermont Holocaust Memorial, that are dedicated to preserving Holocaust history serve as living testaments to
the resilience of the human spirit and the importance of ensuring that such atrocities are never repeated, and
Whereas, the intention of International Holocaust Remembrance Day is to educate the world concerning the
horrors of the Holocaust and, locally, to encourage Vermonters to work together for a society that respects every
individual, that is devoid of all antisemitism, and that prevents the reoccurrence of such a massive human
tragedy, a mission that the Vermont Holocaust Memorial addresses daily through educational and informational
programs, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives
That the General Assembly recognizes January 27, 2026, as International Holocaust Remembrance Day in
Vermont, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to the Vermont Holocaust
Memorial.
