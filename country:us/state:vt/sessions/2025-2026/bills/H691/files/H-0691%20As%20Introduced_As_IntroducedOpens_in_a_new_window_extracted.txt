11 An act relating to breastfeeding education and support
12 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
13 Sec. 1. FINDINGS
14 The General Assembly finds that:
15 (1) Breastfeeding helps protect infants against a range of infections,
16 diseases, and serious outcomes, including sudden infant death syndrome
17 (SIDS).
18 (2) Breast milk adapts to an infant’s needs, is easily digested, and
19 contains immunologic and anti-inflammatory components.

1 (3) Breastfeeding supports cognitive development and reduces the risk

3 (4) Breastfeeding helps a mother’s postpartum recovery and promotes

7 (6) Breastfeeding is more cost-effective for families than purchasing

13 feeding.
14 (8) While breastfeeding is not an option for every mother or family, its

7 Sec. 3. 18 V.S.A. § 1911c is added to read:
8 § 1911c. INFANT-FEEDING POLICY
9 (a) As used in this section:
10 (1) “Baby-Friendly Hospital Initiative” means the program sponsored by
11 the World Health Organization and the United Nations Children’s Fund
12 (UNICEF) that recognizes hospitals that offer an optimal level of care for
13 infant feeding.
14 (2) “Perinatal unit” means a maternity and newborn service of the
15 hospital for the provision of care during pregnancy, labor, delivery, and
16 postpartum and neonatal periods with appropriate staff, space, equipment, and
17 supplies.
18 (b) Each general hospital that has a perinatal unit shall have an infant-
19 feeding policy. The infant-feeding policy shall promote breastfeeding,
20 utilizing guidance provided by the Baby-Friendly Hospital Initiative and the
21 Department of Health. The infant-feeding policy may include guidance on
