15 conduct or to engage in sexual conduct; to extend the statute of limitations for

17 statute; to extend the statute of limitations for displaying or disclosing images
18 in violation of the voyeurism statute; to eliminate the statute of limitations for
19 civil actions against a person for knowingly disclosing, without the plaintiff’s
20 consent, a visual image of the plaintiff while the plaintiff is nude or engaged in
21 sexual conduct if the disclosure caused harm to the plaintiff; and to allow

Sec. 3. 13 V.S.A. § 2607 is added to read:
§ 2607. SEXUAL EXTORTION
(a) As used in this section:
(1) “Nude” has the same meaning as in section 2606 of this title.
(2) “Serious bodily injury” has the same meaning as in section 1021 of
this title.
(3) “Sexual conduct” has the same meaning as in section 2821 of this
title.
(4) “Visual image” has the same meaning as is section 2606 of this title.
(b) No person shall knowingly threaten to disclose a visual image of an
identifiable person who is nude or who is engaged in sexual conduct, without
the person’s consent, with the intent to compel a person to:
(1) produce nude images or images of sexual conduct;
(2) engage in sexual conduct;
(3) engage in any act against the person’s will;
(4) refrain from engaging in any act in which the person has a legal
right to engage; or
(5) provide money or anything of value.
