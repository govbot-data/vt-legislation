Act No. 174 (S.212). An act relating to potable water supply and wastewater system
connections
Subjects: Conservation and development; municipal authority; potable water
supply and wastewater connections
This act requires the Secretary of Natural Resources to adopt a general permit for
potable water supply and wastewater system connections to new or existing structures.
Under the general permit, the Secretary may give deference to applications for
connections certified by a licensed designer. The Secretary shall publish a guidance
manual for licensed designers implementing the general permit. The act also authorizes
the Secretary to adopt a potable water supply and wastewater system general permit for
various categories of permitted activity, including the subdivision of land when no
building, structure, or campground exists on or is proposed for the property at the time of
subdivision; for boundary line adjustments for improved or unimproved lots; for the
permitting of potable water supply systems with a design flow of less than 1,000 gallons
per day when there is no requirement for any variance, hydrogeologic analysis, or yield
testing of a potable water source; and for wastewater systems that have a design flow of
less than 1,000 gallons per day and do not require a variance, a hydrogeologic analysis, or
innovative or alternative technologies. The act establishes lower permitting fees for the
general permit for potable water supply or wastewater system connections, and the act
repeals a permit fee cap for potable water supply or wastewater system projects in certain
designated areas.
The act repeals statutory authority for the Secretary to delegate full potable water
supply and wastewater system permitting to a municipality. The act authorizes the
Secretary to delegate to a municipality partial authority to conduct technical review of
proposed projects that include both municipal potable water supply and municipal
wastewater system connections, provided that the water main and sanitary sewer
collection line connected to them are owned and controlled by the delegated
municipality. A municipality that is delegated connection authority shall incorporate the
requirements of the Secretary’s general permit for potable water supply and wastewater
system connections as part of municipal approval, including deference to applications
certified by a licensed designer.
In addition, the act requires the Secretary of Natural Resources’ Technical Advisory
Committee (TAC) on or before January 15, 2027, to report to the General Assembly
regarding authority under State statute and rules that allows the isolation distances for
potable water supplies and wastewater systems to extend onto neighboring property, a
practice commonly referred to as overshadowing. The TAC shall summarize the
scientific and technical basis and benefit to the environment and public health from
overshadowing; evaluate alternatives to overshadowing that could prevent or
significantly limit overshadowing of property and recommend preferred alternatives;

present data on the frequency of overshadowing and on the different alternatives or types
of outcomes that occur when overshadowing exists; and recommend additional steps the

between neighbors when overshadowing occurs.
The act further authorizes the Secretary to enter into a delegation agreement with the
owner of a publicly owned treatment works (POTW) for the regulation of pretreatment
discharges to the POTW. A pretreatment discharge delegation agreement entered into by
the Secretary shall authorize the owner of the POTW to regulate and enforce pretreatment
discharges to the POTW consistent with federal law. The act provides that the authority
of the Secretary to enter into a pretreatment discharge delegation agreement is contingent
upon the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) notifying the Secretary that
delegation of pretreatment permitting is authorized under a previous agreement between
EPA and the State.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on June 18, 2026
