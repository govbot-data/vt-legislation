Act No. 165 (H.937). An act relating to miscellaneous judiciary procedures
Subjects: Criminal procedure; court procedure; miscellaneous amendments
This act makes a number of changes to court and Judiciary procedures, including
permitting the Attorney General and crime victims to prevent an offender from profiting
from the offender’s criminal conduct; making a technical amendment to the underage
alcohol and cannabis statute to provide automatic license reinstatement after a temporary
suspension for failure to complete the Youth Substance Awareness Safety Program;
permitting public access to criminal case records subject to rules promulgated by the
Judiciary; establishing procedures for renewal of a judgment for money or property in the
Family Division; amending the stalking laws to clarify that the acts required to establish a
course of conduct may occur on the same day; conforming statutes to reflect current
practice regarding notification of crime victims regarding the offender’s location and
permitting crime victims to opt out of notifications from the Department of Corrections;
substituting “health care provider” for “physician” for one of the designated positions on
the Victims Compensation Board; amending the law regarding the length of time for
keeping confidential pretrial risk assessments and needs screenings from three years to
two years to conform to the general State policy of two years minimum; repealing the
statutes related to “spendthrift guardianships”; making conforming changes to
computation of court deadlines; removing the requirement that a certified copy of a child
custody determination be mailed to the court administrator because such determinations
are direct filed; providing that certain deeds establish joint tenancy and not tenancy in
common; increasing the valuation of lost property that requires posting by the person
who found the property from $3.00 to $50.00; establishing procedures and protocols for
rapid accountability dockets; amending the home and land improvement fraud statute to
conform to recent court decisions; clarifying that the liability caps applicable to federal
Section 1983 actions under existing law in the Vermont Tort Claims Act also apply to the
new state cause of action created in 2026 Acts and Resolves No. 87, which is modeled
after 42 U.S.C. § 1983; updating language to reflect the delayed start of the Tenant
Representation Pilot Program and expanding the Pilot Program statewide; authorizing the
Human Rights Commission (HRC) to disclose investigative reports resulting from a
complaint initiated by an HRC employee; permitting the court to issue a domestic relief
from abuse order if the defendant is under the supervision of the Department of
Corrections for certain violent crimes, not just if the defendant is incarcerated; and
making various technical and conforming changes to statutes involving court process.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on June 18, 2026
