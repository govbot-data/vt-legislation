Act No. 130 (H.542). An act relating to testing of schools in Vermont for polychlorinated
biphenyls
Subjects: Education; conservation and development; testing; polychlorinated
biphenyls
This act extends from July 1, 2027, to August 1, 2035, the deadline by which public
schools or recognized independent schools constructed or renovated prior to 1980 are
required to test for polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs) in indoor air. The act also creates
the School Polychlorinated Biphenyl Program Fund to provide grants from the Agency of
Natural Resources (ANR) for the investigation, mitigation, and remediation of PCBs in
schools in Vermont. The Fund would consist of any litigation recovery by the State for
the costs of addressing PCB contamination in schools and reimbursements from a school
for work related to a grant issued by the State for PCB investigation, mitigation, and
remediation when that school recovers money from litigation or other awards. In
addition, the act amends the School Construction Aid Program to provide that in order for
the Secretary of Education to approve the preliminary application for school construction
aid, an applicant school district applying for aid for a school building that was
constructed or renovated before 1980 shall demonstrate that indoor air quality testing for
PCBs has been conducted at the school. Last, the act requires ANR, on or before January
15, 2027, to submit to the General Assembly an estimate of the additional cost to the
State to complete testing, mitigation, and remediation for PCBs at public schools and
approved and recognized independent schools and a plan to fund the costs estimated
necessary to complete testing, mitigation, and remediation.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on June 15, 2026
