AS PASSED BY HOUSE AND SENATE H.817
2026 Page 1 of 2
1 H.817
2 An act relating to mental health literacy and peer-to-peer supports in
3 schools
4 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
5 Sec. 1. LEGISLATIVE INTENT; MENTAL HEALTH LITERACY AND
6 PEER SUPPORT INITIATIVES
7 It is the intent of the General Assembly that the Department of Mental
8 Health, in collaboration with the Agency of Education, shall oversee a mental
9 health literacy training grant program to provide mental health literacy training
10 to educators, other school personnel, and afterschool staff and a peer-to-peer
11 mental health program to support structured opportunities for student peer
12 connection in a supervised school or afterschool setting to take effect on July
13 1, 2028.
14 Sec. 2. FUNDING; MENTAL HEALTH LITERACY AND PEER SUPPORT
15 INITIATIVES
16 As part of its fiscal year 2028 budget presentation, the Department of
17 Mental Health, in collaboration with the Agency of Education, shall explore
18 potential funding sources for the mental health literacy and peer-to-peer
19 programming described in Sec. 1 of this act, including whether any existing
20 special funds are appropriate sources of funding, and provide
21 recommendations accordingly.

1 Sec. 3. INVENTORY; MENTAL HEALTH LITERACY AND PEER
2 SUPPORT INITIATIVES
3 On or before January 15, 2027, the Department of Mental Health, in
4 collaboration with the Agency of Education and stakeholders, shall submit an
5 inventory of existing mental health programming and services in schools to the
6 House Committees on Appropriations and on Health Care and to the Senate
7 Committees on Appropriations and on Health and Welfare. The inventory
8 shall include a recommendation to integrate the mental health literacy and
9 peer-to-peer programming described in Sec. 1 of this act into the existing
10 programming and services listed in the inventory required pursuant to this
11 section.
12 Sec. 4. EFFECTIVE DATE
13 This act shall take effect on July 1, 2026.
