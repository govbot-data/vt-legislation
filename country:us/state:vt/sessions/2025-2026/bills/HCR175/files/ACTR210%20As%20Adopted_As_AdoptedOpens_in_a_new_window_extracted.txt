Whereas, Stan Baker, whose forebears were Green Mountain Boys and whose names
are reflected in the towns Bakersfield and Stannard, wrote his own page of Vermont
history, and
Whereas, a Swarthmore College graduate, Stan Baker was a co-founder of and teacher
at the Otter Creek School, a registered play therapist, a licensed mental health counselor,
and a mental health clinical director in Addison and Chittenden Counties, and
Whereas, in the 1990s, he and his life partner, Peter Harrigan, Professor of Fine Arts:
Theater at St. Michael’s College, desired to marry, but as a same-gender couple this was
legally impossible, and
Whereas, in partnership with two other LGBTQ+ couples, they sued for the right to
marry, and in 1999, the Vermont Supreme Court, in the landmark decision, Baker v.
State, 170 Vt. 194, established marriage equality under the Vermont Constitution, and the
holding lead to Act 91 of 2000 establishing civil unions, and ultimately, Act 3 of 2009
authorizing civil marriage, and
Whereas, in 2009, Stan Baker, a strong justice advocate, was ordained as a deacon in
the Episcopal Church, and his local church leadership included service as a chaplain at

Fletcher Allen Health Care (now the University of Vermont Medical Center), a deacon of

Whereas, in June 2025, Stan Baker died at 79 years of age, and his survivors include

Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly extends its sincere condolences to the family and friends
of the Venerable J. Stannard Baker of Shelburne, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to
members of the family of Stan Baker and to the Episcopal Diocese of Vermont.
