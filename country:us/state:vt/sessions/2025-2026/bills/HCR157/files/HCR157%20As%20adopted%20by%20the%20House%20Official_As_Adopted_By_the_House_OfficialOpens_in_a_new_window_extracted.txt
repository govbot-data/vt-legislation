State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 157
Offered by Representatives Masland of Thetford and Holcombe of Norwich
Offered by Senators Clarkson, Major, and White
House concurrent resolution congratulating the Thetford Academy Panthers on winning a sixth consecutive
Division III boys’ track and field championship and for their victories and record-setting performances at the
2025 New England Interscholastic Outdoor Track and Field Championship
Whereas, as the Division III title holders for the past five seasons, the Panthers were well-suited to extend
their victory streak in the championship boys’ track and field competition, and
Whereas, at the 2025 decisive meet held at the D.G. Weaver Athletic Complexin Burlington, Thetford
Academy athletes scored first in the 100-meter, 400-meter, 800-meter, and 1,500-meter dashes; the 4x100 and
4x800 relays; and the long jump, triple jump, discus, and javelin events, and
Whereas, this extensive set of wins enabled the Panthers to amass 164.5 points, far ahead of runner-up White
River Valley’s 65.5 points, for a spectacular sixth consecutive championship triumph, and
Whereas, an exciting coda to the 2025 season was Thetford Academy’s largest ever presence at the New
England Interscholastic Outdoor Track and Field Championship, where the Panthers set three new Vermont
State records, and for the first time earned two championship medals in the long jump and 200–meter dash, and
Whereas, the outstanding Panthers roster featured Hunter Clay, Connor Gaine, Uly Junker-Boyce, Miguel
Kutter-Walker, Luke Miller, Liam Murphy, Hardy Payson, Oliver Ransom, Evan Rich, Bryce Rikert, Brady
Sloop, Ramona Spellman, David Stangner, Matthew Vivian, Isaac Yukica, Wendell Durham, Daniel Mann,
Elijah Renner, Emmett Terry, David Thaxton, Henry Trage, Oliver Emery, Tanner Hardy, Paul Kidd, Jesse
Martin, Benjamin Nalette, Kit Payson, Sebastian Perdrizet, Jacob Phelps, Connor Sloop, Jay Hill, David
Mitchell, Eli Murphy, and Stone Riegler, and
Whereas, co-head coaches Joel Breakstone and Emily Silver; assistant coaches Cole Chapman, MaxHiggins,
Charlie Buttrey, Charlize Brown, and Vanessa Riegler; and Team Manager Twyla Weinstein were all thrilled
with the Panthers’ stupendous success in 2025, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly congratulates the Thetford Academy Panthers on winning a sixth consecutive
Division III boys’ track and field championship and for their victories and record-setting performances at the
2025 New England Interscholastic Outdoor Track and Field Championship, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Thetford Academy.
