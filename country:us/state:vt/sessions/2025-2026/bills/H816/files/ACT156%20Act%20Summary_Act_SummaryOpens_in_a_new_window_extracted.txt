Act No. 156 (H.816). An act relating to regulating the use of artificial intelligence in the
provision of mental health services
Subjects: Health; mental health; professional regulation; artificial intelligence;
therapeutic services
This act prohibits a corporation or entity from providing, advertising, other otherwise
offering mental health services, including through the use of artificial intelligence, to the
public unless the mental health services are either provided by a mental health
professional or part of an approved institutional review board or privacy board study. It
does not preclude a mental health professional who is operating within the professional’s
scope of practice from utilizing Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act–
compliant artificial intelligence tools if the mental health professional first reviews and
approves any mental health services, including software-based medical products
authorized or approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration where the product’s
use is prescribed or recommended by a mental health professional.
Violations of this act by a corporation or entity are enforceable under the Consumer
Protection Act. This act also specifies that engaging in the prohibited use of artificial
intelligence by a mental health professional is enforceable under either the Office of
Professional Regulation’s or the Board of Medical Practice’s unprofessional conduct
statutes, respectively.
This act adds the Director of Professional Regulation or designee and the Executive
Director of the Vermont Board of Medical Practice or designee to the membership of the
Artificial Intelligence Advisory Council. Lastly, it requires the Artificial Intelligence
Advisory Council to submit a report to the General Assembly by January 15, 2027,
regarding the regulation of the use of artificial intelligence by mental health
professionals.
Effective Date: June 17, 2026
