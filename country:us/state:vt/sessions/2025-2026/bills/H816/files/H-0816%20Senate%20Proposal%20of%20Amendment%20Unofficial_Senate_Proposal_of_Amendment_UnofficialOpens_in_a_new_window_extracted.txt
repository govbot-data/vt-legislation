SENATE PROPOSAL OF AMENDMENT H.816
2026 Page 1 of 5
1 H.816
2 An act relating to regulating the use of artificial intelligence in the provision
3 of mental health services
4 The Senate proposes to the House to amend the bill by striking out all after
5 the enacting clause and inserting in lieu thereof the following:
6 Sec. 1. PURPOSE
7 It is the purpose of this act to safeguard individuals seeking mental health
8 services in Vermont from psychological harm, including death by suicide, by
9 ensuring that these services are delivered by mental health professionals and
10 not independently by artificial intelligence systems.
11 Sec. 2. 3 V.S.A. § 129a is amended to read:
12 § 129a. UNPROFESSIONAL CONDUCT
13 (a) In addition to any other provision of law, the following conduct by a
14 licensee constitutes unprofessional conduct. When that conduct is by an
15 applicant or person who later becomes an applicant, it may constitute grounds
16 for denial of a license or other disciplinary action. Any one of the following
17 items or any combination of items, whether the conduct at issue was
18 committed within or outside the State, shall constitute unprofessional conduct:
19 * * *
20 (30) For any mental health professional, engaging in the prohibited use
21 of artificial intelligence pursuant to 18 V.S.A. § 7115.

2 Sec. 3. 18 V.S.A. § 7115 is added to read:
3 § 7115. PROHIBITED USES OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE
4 (a) As used in this section:
5 (1) “Artificial intelligence” means an engineered or machine-based
6 system that varies in its level of autonomy and that can, for explicit or implicit
7 objectives, infer from the input it receives how to generate outputs that can
8 influence physical or virtual environments.
9 (2) “Mental health professional” means an individual licensed, certified,
10 or rostered, respectively, to provide mental health services as a physician
11 pursuant to 26 V.S.A. chapter 23 or 33; an advanced practice registered nurse
12 specializing in psychiatric mental health pursuant to 26 V.S.A. chapter 28; a
13 psychologist pursuant to 26 V.S.A. chapter 55; a peer support provider or peer
14 recovery support specialist pursuant to 26 V.S.A. chapter 60; a social worker
15 pursuant to 26 V.S.A. chapter 61; an alcohol and drug abuse counselor
16 pursuant to 26 V.S.A. chapter 62; a clinical mental health counselor pursuant
17 to 26 V.S.A. chapter 65; a marriage and family therapist pursuant to 26 V.S.A
18 chapter 76; a psychoanalyst pursuant to 26 V.S.A. chapter 77; an applied
19 behavior analyst pursuant to 26 V.S.A. chapter 95; a nonlicensed or
20 noncertified psychotherapist or a noncertified psychoanalyst; or any other
21 professional who provides mental health services.

1 (3) “Mental health services” means counseling, therapy, or
2 psychotherapy services used to diagnose or treat an individual’s mental or
3 behavioral health or provide ongoing recovery support, including providing
4 therapeutic decisions, issuing direct therapeutic communications, generating
5 treatment plans or recommendations, or detecting or interpreting emotion or
6 mental states.
7 (4) “Therapeutic communication” means a written, verbal, or nonverbal
8 interaction intended to diagnose or treat any type of mental or behavioral
9 health concern, provide ongoing recovery support, or provide any advice
10 related to diagnosis, treatment, or recovery, such as:
11 (A) engaging in direct interactions with clients or patients for the
12 purpose of understanding or reflecting the client’s or patient’s thoughts;
13 (B) providing guidance, therapeutic strategies, or interventions
14 designed to achieve mental health outcomes;
15 (C) offering emotional support, reassurance, or empathy in response
16 to emotional or psychological distress;
17 (D) collaborating with a patient or client to develop or modify
18 treatment plans or therapeutic goals; and
19 (E) delivering feedback intended to promote growth or address
20 mental health outcomes.

1 (5) “Therapeutic decision” means the final clinical determination
2 regarding diagnosis or the selection, modification, or termination of treatment
3 or care.
4 (b) A corporation or entity shall not provide, advertise, or otherwise offer
5 mental health services, including through the use of artificial intelligence, to
6 the public unless the mental health services are provided by a mental health
7 professional.
8 (c)(1) A violation of this section by a corporation or entity shall be deemed
9 a violation of the Consumer Protection Act, 9 V.S.A. chapter 63. The Attorney
10 General has the same authority, and private parties have the same rights and
11 remedies, as provided under 9 V.S.A. chapter 63, subchapter 1. Each violation
12 of this section shall carry a civil penalty of $10,000.00 as set forth in 9 V.S.A.
13 § 2461.
14 (2) Nothing in this section shall be construed to preclude or supplant any
15 other statutory or common law remedies.
16 (d) Nothing in this section shall preclude a mental health professional who
17 is operating within the professional’s scope of practice from utilizing artificial
18 intelligence tools that are compliant with the Health Insurance Portability and
19 Accountability Act of 1996, Pub. L. No. 104-191, provided that the mental
20 health professional reviews and approves any mental health services.
