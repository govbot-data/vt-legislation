Act No. 76 (H.545). An act relating to issuing immunization recommendations
Subjects: Health; liability; public health; immunizations; recommendations;
standing orders; purchasing authority; insurance overage
This act temporarily pauses the State’s reliance on immunization schedules
recommended by the Centers for Disease Control’s Advisory Committee on
Immunization Practices. Instead, it grants the Commissioner of Health time-limited
authority to issue recommended immunization schedules for both adults and children
after consulting with the Vermont Immunization Advisory Council and reviewing the
recommendations of various professional organizations, such as the Centers for Disease
Control’s Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices, the American Academy of
Pediatrics, and the American Academy of Family Physicians. This act authorizes the
Commissioner to issue standing orders to health care professionals to prescribe, dispense,
and administer immunizations recommended by the Commissioner to the extent that
doing so is within the health care professional’s scope of practice. It further provides
immunity from civil and administrative liability to a health care professional who
prescribes, dispenses, or administers an immunization recommended by the
Commissioner for an immunization-caused adverse event, unless the health care
professional’s acts constituted gross negligence, recklessness, or intentional misconduct.
This act makes temporary changes to statutes addressing group health insurance
policies, health benefit plans for individual and small group markets, and the regulation
of pharmacists where these statutes previously either tied coverage for immunizations or
pharmacy protocols to recommendations issued by the Advisory Committee on
Immunization Practices. The statutes are amended to return to their prior configuration
on July 1, 2031, when the Commissioner’s authority to issue recommended immunization
schedules under this act is repealed.
Multiple effective dates, beginning on March 24, 2026
