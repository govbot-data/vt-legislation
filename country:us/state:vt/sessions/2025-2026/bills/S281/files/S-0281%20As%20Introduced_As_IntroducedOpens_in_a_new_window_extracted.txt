10 An act relating to the Zero-Carbon Nuclear Feasibility Study for Vermont
11 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
12 Sec. 1. VERMONT ZERO-CARBON NUCLEAR FEASIBILITY STUDY
13 (a) Creation. There is created the Nuclear Power Study Committee to
14 identify the obstacles to constructing a new nuclear energy generation facility
15 in Vermont, including those in State and federal law.
16 (b) Membership. The Study Committee shall have seven members
17 appointed as follows:
18 (1) the Chair of the Public Utility Commission or designee;
19 (2) a representative from the Vermont Electric Power Company;
20 (3) a representative from ISO New England Inc.;

4 (5) two current members of the Senate, each from different political
5 parties, who shall be appointed by the Committee on Committees.
6 (c) Powers and duties. The Study Committee shall:
7 (1) review reasons why the prior nuclear generation facility closed, what
8 obstacles exist in both State and federal law, and what practical issues may
9 exist that would prevent a new facility from opening; and
10 (2) hear from a variety of stakeholders on this issue, including
11 representatives of electric utilities, representatives from NextEra Energy,
12 representatives from the Nuclear Energy Institute, and representatives from the
13 U.S. Department of Energy.
14 (d) Assistance. The Study Committee shall have the administrative
15 assistance of the Office of Legislative Operations, the legal assistance of the
16 Office of Legislative Counsel, and the analytical assistance of the Joint Fiscal
17 Office.
18 (e) Meetings.
19 (1) The Office of Legislative Counsel shall call the first meeting of the
20 Study Committee to occur on or before July 31, 2026.
