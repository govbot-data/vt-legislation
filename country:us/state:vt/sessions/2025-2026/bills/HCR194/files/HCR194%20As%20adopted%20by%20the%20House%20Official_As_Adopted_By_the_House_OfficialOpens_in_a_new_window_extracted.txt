State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 194
Offered by Representatives Morrissey of Bennington, Greer of Bennington, Corcoran of Bennington, and
Nigro of Bennington
Offered by Senators Bongartz and Plunkett
House concurrent resolution in memory of North Bennington educator, coach, volunteer firefighter, and
religious leader Robert P. Nolan
Whereas, Bob Nolan graduated from North Bennington High School, attended the University of Vermont,
earned a bachelor’s degree in education from the former Castleton State College, and served in the U.S.
Army, and
Whereas, for three decades, he was a respected sixth-grade teacher at North Bennington Elementary School,
but his involvement with local youth extended beyond the classroom to many athletic fields, drawing on his
experience as a college sportsman, and
Whereas, at North Bennington High School, Bob Nolan coached basketball, cross-country, and track, and
aside from school sports, he coached PONY league baseball, assisted with American Legion Baseball, and
organized church youth group basketball competitions, and
Whereas, perhaps Bob Nolan’s most impactful civic role in North Bennington was his 70-year volunteer
membership in the North Bennington Fire Department, including 40 years as the organization’s dedicated
president, overseeing administrative matters, dousing many a blaze, and attending to other emergencies, and
Whereas, his leadership roles at the North Bennington Congregational Church included trustee, deacon,
moderator, and occasionally serving as the congregation’s lay minister, and
Whereas, later in life, Bob Nolan developed a second career as a house painter, and
Whereas, he loved to hunt and fish, was actively involved as both a volunteer and manager in the
preservation and supervision of Lake Paran as a community resource, and was a member of both the Loyal
Order of Moose and the American Legion, and
Whereas, Bob Nolan, a cherished citizen of North Bennington, and a man with a great sense of humor who
truly made the village a better place to live, died at 93 years of age on October 11, 2025, and his survivors
include his wife of 72 years, Florence; his children; grandchildren; great-grandchildren; and great-great
grandchild, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly expresses its sincere sympathy to the family of Robert P. Nolan, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to the family of Bob
Nolan.
