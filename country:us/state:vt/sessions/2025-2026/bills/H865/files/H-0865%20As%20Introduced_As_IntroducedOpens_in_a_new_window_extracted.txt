1 (a) A public servant shall file with the Ethics Commission a disclosure
2 detailing costs and associated information for any travel made in course of the
3 public servant’s official capacity or because of the public servant’s status as a
4 public servant. As used in this section, traveling “because of a public servant’s
5 status” means that the travel would not have likely occurred but for the public
6 servant’s status as a public servant.
7 (b) A public servant shall disclose and designate:
8 (1) whether the travel was purely in the public servant’s official capacity
9 or a mixture of official and private purposes;
10 (2) any destination of travel, including any stopover or intentional visit
11 to another location prior to the destination of travel;
12 (3) the declared purpose of the travel;
13 (4) all costs associated with transportation to and from any destination,
14 and food, refreshments, tickets and admissions, entertainment, lodging, and
15 anything else of value, whether for cost or in-kind, associated with the travel;
16 (5) which elements of associated travel costs were paid for or provided
17 by:
18 (A) the State;
19 (B) the public servant’s own person; or
20 (C) any other sources, including associations, lobbyists, political
21 committees and parties, and individuals; and

1 (6) an attestation to the veracity and completeness of the disclosed
2 information.
3 (c) A public servant shall file the disclosure with the Ethics Commission
4 within 30 calendar days after beginning the travel. If travel continues for more
5 than 30 calendar days, the public servant shall supplement the disclosure on a
6 weekly basis.
7 (d) Notwithstanding subsections (a)–(c) of this section, a public servant
8 shall not file a disclosure for any travel that constitutes regular commuting to
9 and from the public servant’s official place or places of work.
10 Sec. 2. EFFECTIVE DATE
11 This act shall take effect on July 1, 2026.
