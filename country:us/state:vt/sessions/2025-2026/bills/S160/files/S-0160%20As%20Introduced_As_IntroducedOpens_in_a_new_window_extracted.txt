5 Subject: Criminal and civil procedures; firearms
6 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to prohibit the
7 possession of machine guns and rapid-fire devices that permit a weapon to
8 function as or mimic a machine gun.
9 An act relating to firearms procedures
10 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
11 Sec. 1. 13 V.S.A. § 4022 is amended to read:
12 § 4022. BUMP-FIRE STOCKS; MACHINE GUNS; RAPID-FIRE
13 DEVICES; POSSESSION PROHIBITED
14 (a) As used in this section,:
15 (1) “bump-fire stock” “Bump-fire stock” means a butt stock designed to
16 be attached to a semiautomatic firearm and intended to increase the rate of fire
17 achievable with the firearm to that of a fully automatic firearm by using the
18 energy from the recoil of the firearm to generate a reciprocating action that
19 facilitates the repeated activation of the trigger.

1 (2) “Auto sear” means a device, other than a bump stock, designed to
2 convert a semiautomatic firearm to shoot more than one shot with a single pull
3 of the trigger without manual reloading.
4 (3) “Machine gun” means any weapon that shoots, is designed to shoot,
5 or can be readily restored to shoot automatically more than one shot without
6 manual reloading, by a single function of the trigger. “Machine gun” also
7 includes the frame or receiver of any such weapon; any part designed and
8 intended solely and exclusively, or combination of parts designed and
9 intended, for use in converting a weapon into a machine gun; and any
10 combination of parts from which a machine gun can be assembled if the parts
11 are in the possession or under the control of a person.
12 (4) “Rapid-fire device” means any device, including an auto sear, that
13 when installed in or attached to a firearm increases the weapon’s rate of fire or
14 the rate at which the trigger is activated.
15 (b) A person shall not possess a bump-fire stock, machine gun, or rapid-fire
16 device. A person who violates this subsection shall be imprisoned not more
17 than one year or fined not more than $1,000.00, or both.
18 (c) The Department of Public Safety shall develop, promote, and execute a
19 collection process that permits persons to voluntarily and anonymously
20 relinquish bump-fire stocks prior to the effective date of this section October 1,
21 2018.
